Orestis II

Orestis II of Greece - born as Orestis Mastakas and widely known as Orestis the Great - is a claimant to the throne of Greece, founder of Orestisism, and architect of the vision for a Fourth Hellenic Civilization. This website serves as the official archive of the Royal Household of Orestis II, documenting his life, campaigns, philosophy, media presence, political doctrine, and long-term program for the restoration and renewal of Greece.

Orestis II emerged through academic distinction, student political activism, institutional reform efforts, and philosophical writing. His work spans university governance, public policy, economic thought, national strategy, and civilizational theory. Central to his philosophy is Orestisism: a doctrine centered on meritocracy, stewardship, technological progress, national renewal, and the long-term development of a Fourth Hellenic Civilization: a renewed Greek state combining institutional competence, scientific excellence, demographic vitality, strategic sovereignty, and cultural confidence.
