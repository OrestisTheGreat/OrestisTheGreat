The Crisis of the Present System

For decades, Greece has experienced the same cycle. Governments change. Parties change. Promises change. Yet the structural problems remain.

Citizens have witnessed declining trust in institutions, repeated failures without meaningful accountability, and a political system often focused more on short-term survival than long-term national interest.

They have seen ministers entrusted with responsibilities for which they possess little relevant expertise - sometimes without even holding a university degree - while political loyalty and communication skills often appear to matter more than demonstrated competence.

The consequences reach every part of national life.

Families face rising costs of living, declining purchasing power, and increasing difficulty achieving economic stability. Young Greeks leave the country in search of opportunities elsewhere, creating demographic decline and a loss of national talent.

The economy remains constrained by bureaucracy, low productivity, insufficient innovation, and difficulty creating high-value industries capable of competing internationally.

Public services and infrastructure continue to suffer from inefficiency, delayed reform, and weak institutional capacity.

The Core Diagnosis

The problem is not simply the failure of individual politicians or parties. Individuals and parties change; the pattern remains.

A system that rewards popularity over competence, party loyalty over ability, and electoral calculation over national strategy will continue producing governments unable to address challenges that require decades of preparation.

Greece requires more than reform. It requires new institutions, a new political order, and a new national direction. It requires a state where responsibility is matched with competence, where leadership is selected according to ability and integrity, and where national priorities are pursued beyond short-term political competition.

It requires a national strategy focused not merely on managing decline, but on rebuilding strength, confidence, sovereignty, and ambition—a strategy measured in decades rather than electoral cycles.

This is why Orestisism exists. This is the purpose of the Restoration Project.

Join the Restoration Project

Orestisism cannot remain only an intellectual framework.

Ideas do not transform nations by themselves. They require people willing to defend them, organize around them, and turn them into action.

Every political transformation begins as a small community of people who refuse to believe that decline is inevitable. This movement begins with people like them.

Agreement with every proposal is not required. Serious discussion, honest criticism, and a willingness to engage with the difficult questions facing Greece are essential.

Students, scholars, engineers, entrepreneurs, public servants, military personnel, artists, and citizens from every field of society have a role to play. Whether through political activity, research, public advocacy, cultural work, or practical organization, every contribution strengthens the Restoration Project.

Politics is chess played across generations. Take your place at the board.
