For Those Who Think in Generations

Every civilization begins as an idea before it becomes an institution.

The Fourth Hellenic Civilization Begins Small

The ideas presented throughout this archive were developed over many years through study, political activity, public service, and discussion.

The project remains in its early stages, but its ambitions extend far beyond a single lifetime.

The restoration of a civilization is not the work of one individual. It requires citizens, scholars, engineers, entrepreneurs, public servants, artists, and builders willing to think beyond electoral cycles and beyond their own generation.

The Royal Chancellery

Visitors occasionally inquire about the Royal Chancellery, the Court Historians, and the institutions referenced throughout this archive.

At present, these bodies consist primarily of a small circle of friends, supporters, collaborators, and occasional volunteers who have contributed ideas, criticism, research, editing, documentation, and encouragement to the broader project. The terminology reflects both the constitutional-monarchical framework of Orestisism and the unusual traditions that have gradually formed around this project over time.

Join the Movement

Orestisism does not require perfect agreement.

Curiosity, seriousness, and a willingness to think beyond short-term political cycles are sufficient.

This project welcomes all those willing to contribute to the long-term restoration of Greece.

Students, workers, entrepreneurs, scholars, soldiers, public servants, artists - all have a role to play.

Some will contribute through policy and institutional design. Others through organization, campaigning, public advocacy, and cultural renewal.

The restoration of a civilization requires hard work at every level.
