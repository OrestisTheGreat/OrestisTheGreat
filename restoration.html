National Restoration Program

A Hundred-Year Plan Toward the Fourth Hellenic Civilization

The Restoration Program is the strategic blueprint of Orestis II for the long-term renewal of Greece.

Its objective is not merely economic recovery, but the reconstruction of state capacity, demographic vitality, technological strength, strategic sovereignty, and civilizational confidence.

Strategic Horizons

The program is divided into three strategic horizons:

  • Immediate Horizon (0–5 years): Stabilization and state reform.
  • Strategic Horizon (5–20 years): National transformation.
  • Civilizational Horizon (20–100 years): The Fourth Hellenic Civilization.
I. Constitutional and Institutional Reform

The greatest weakness of modern Greece is institutional rather than civilizational. The state remains excessively reactive, bureaucratic, and constrained by short electoral cycles.

Restoration begins with the creation of institutions capable of long-term strategic governance.

Constitutional Order

  • Establish a mixed constitutional system combining democratic participation, technocratic administration, constitutional continuity, and long-term strategic governance.
  • Long-term objective: the creation of a modern Res Publica model balancing popular sovereignty, institutional competence, and civilizational continuity.
  • Adopt a doctrine of National Historical Mission, whereby the state pursues objectives extending across decades and centuries rather than electoral cycles.
  • Create Strategic Constitutional Authorities responsible for safeguarding long-term national priorities, including demographics, energy, artificial intelligence, defense, water security, and other domains designated by constitutional law.
  • Select members of the Strategic Constitutional Authorities through national examinations, integrity reviews, and mission-alignment assessments. Require approval by the Constitutional Monarch. Provide long, staggered terms to ensure continuity and independence.
  • Within their constitutionally defined jurisdictions, Strategic Constitutional Authorities shall possess authority superior to ordinary legislation and executive directives. Their mandate shall be to preserve and advance the long-term interests of the Fourth Hellenic Civilization.
  • The mission, jurisdiction, and constitutional powers of the Strategic Constitutional Authorities may be amended only through extraordinary constitutional procedures requiring a supermajority of the national legislature.
  • Introduce permanent online referendums within clearly defined constitutional limits. Citizens may propose and approve legislation directly in areas not reserved to the Strategic Constitutional Authorities.
  • Ultimate sovereignty resides in the Hellenic people. Democratic institutions, strategic institutions, and constitutional continuity shall function as complementary sources of legitimacy.
  • The Constitutional Monarch serves as guardian of constitutional continuity, national unity, and emergency state authority. During war, constitutional breakdown, or the inability to form a government, emergency executive authority may temporarily pass to the Crown under conditions established by constitutional law.
  • The reigning Monarch may nominate a successor. Such nomination shall require approval by both the national legislature and the Strategic Constitutional Authorities before taking effect.

State Administration

  • Ministers and senior ministry officials shall be selected through competitive national examinations rather than partisan appointment.
  • Preparation materials, examination standards, and evaluation criteria shall be publicly available.
  • Any citizen possessing full civic status may participate in the relevant examinations.
  • The role of ministers is administrative rather than political. Ministers shall not determine national policy but shall execute constitutional objectives, legislation, and government directives with maximum efficiency and competence.
  • The elected government shall retain responsibility for legislation, political direction, and the establishment of measurable national objectives.
  • Each ministry shall operate according to performance targets established by law or government policy.
  • Officials who consistently fail to achieve established objectives shall be removed from office.
  • Promotion and advancement shall be based on measurable outcomes, demonstrated competence, and public service performance rather than tenure, personal connections, or partisan affiliation.

Anti-Corruption and Public Accountability

  • Establish an independent auditing authority responsible for monitoring politicians, judges, and ministers.
  • Select members through Venetian-style sortition among qualified citizens with no active political affiliations.
  • Create a second auditing body to oversee and monitor the first.
  • Implement continuous oversight of financial transactions involving elected officials and senior public office holders.

Strategic Analysis

  • Establish a National Forecasting Office responsible for long-term demographic, economic, technological, and geopolitical analysis.
  • Require periodic evaluation of major public policies according to measurable national objectives.
  • Expand government data collection while protecting information security.
  • Require strategic scenario planning for major national risks, including war, demographic decline, technological disruption, climate change, financial crises, and supply-chain disruptions.

Judicial Reform

  • Appoint judges through impartial Venetian-style selection procedures designed to maximize independence.
  • Accelerate legal proceedings through strict procedural deadlines.
  • Fully digitalize judicial administration, including submissions and case management.
  • Require transitional periods and stability guarantees for legislation affecting taxation, business licensing, and investment.
  • Conduct periodic reviews to eliminate obsolete laws and regulations.
  • Require regulatory-impact assessments for major legislation.
  • Limit parliamentary immunity to acts directly connected with legislative functions.

Public Order and Internal Security

  • Expand the deployment of public security cameras in high-risk urban areas to deter crime and improve investigative capacity.
  • Integrate surveillance systems under a strict legal framework governing data retention, access control, and judicial oversight.
  • Strengthen measures against organized crime, trafficking networks, corruption, and repeat violent offenders.
  • Introduce citizen whistleblower incentives for reporting serious unlawful activity.
  • Prevent the occupation, vandalism, or unlawful disruption of educational and public facilities.
  • Establish permanent security capabilities for university campuses under clearly defined legal authority.
  • Suppress hooligan violence through stricter penalties, enhanced event security, and stronger accountability for sports organizations.
  • Counter political violence, extremist violence, and organized attempts to undermine constitutional order.
  • Distinguish rehabilitation pathways for low-risk offenders while maintaining severe penalties for violent and organized criminal activity.

Local Governance

  • Elect local officials through a combination of public vote and competency assessment.
  • Expand local authority over matters of regional concern.
  • Subject local governments to oversight by an independent auditing branch.

Digital State Completion

  • Create a unified citizen account for all government services.
  • Eliminate paper-based certificates exchanged between public agencies.
  • Impose strict service-delivery deadlines throughout the public administration.
  • Reduce administrative infrastructure costs through digital service delivery and remote work arrangements.
  • Use teleworking to support population distribution and regional development objectives.
  • Simplify administrative procedures through plain-language legislation and standardized digital processes.
II. National Strategy and Sovereignty

A state that cannot defend its interests cannot secure its future. Greece must regain strategic autonomy while remaining realistic about geopolitical constraints.

Foreign Policy

  • Greece shall maintain relations with all major powers while preserving maximum strategic independence.
  • Maintain short- and medium-term alignment with the European Union and NATO while gradually increasing strategic autonomy.
  • Renegotiate or withdraw from selected European treaties, particularly in immigration and environmental policy.
  • Develop strategic partnerships with European states that support greater national sovereignty and institutional flexibility within the European framework.
  • Pursue long-term alignment with rising non-Western powers, including China.
  • Extend Greek territorial waters to twelve nautical miles regardless of external opposition.
  • Prepare for the possibility of future military confrontation with Turkey.
  • Expand strategic intelligence, counter-intelligence, and influence capabilities in support of national security and foreign policy objectives.
  • Revive the Megali Idea as a strategic and cultural counterweight to Turkish expansionism.

Hellenic Diaspora Strategy

  • Encourage the return of highly skilled members of the diaspora.
  • Support Greek-language and cultural education among diaspora communities.
  • Encourage voluntary settlement, investment, and economic activity by Greeks in historically Hellenic regions.

Maritime Strategy

  • Strengthen the protection and monitoring of territorial waters, exclusive economic zones, and critical maritime infrastructure.
  • Expand maritime domain awareness through naval, aerial, space-based, and digital surveillance capabilities.
  • Develop a domestic naval-industrial sector capable of designing, constructing, maintaining, and modernizing warships and autonomous maritime systems.
  • Prioritize technologies that strengthen maritime denial, coastal defense, and island security.
  • Reduce long-term dependence on foreign suppliers for critical naval capabilities.
  • Maintain control of critical sea lines of communication and island approaches.
  • Deny hostile forces freedom of action within the Aegean and Eastern Mediterranean.
  • Integrate naval, air, missile, cyber, and unmanned systems into a unified maritime-defense network.
  • Emphasize submarines, missiles, drones, autonomous systems, and distributed naval power. Avoid dependence on expensive power-projection platforms unsuited to Greek geography.

Island Development Strategy

  • Prioritize energy independence, water security, healthcare access, and digital connectivity for island communities.
  • Encourage permanent settlement and economic activity in sparsely populated islands.
  • Strengthen transportation links between island regions and the mainland.
  • Integrate island development into national defense, maritime strategy, and demographic planning.
  • Expand strategic infrastructure capable of supporting both civilian and defense requirements.
  • Develop selected islands as centers for maritime research, aquaculture, renewable energy, and advanced logistics.

Defense Reform

  • Greece shall maintain military capabilities sufficient to defend national sovereignty, deter aggression, protect Hellenic interests, and exploit favorable strategic opportunities when historical circumstances permit.
  • Prioritize domestic defense production and self-sufficiency.
  • Emphasize drones, autonomous systems, and cost-effective weapon platforms.
  • Ensure the continued functioning of the state during war through redundant energy systems, dispersed industrial capacity, strategic reserves, secure communications, cyber-defense capabilities, and continuity-of-government planning.
  • Develop a strategic destruction doctrine: any attack upon Greece shall trigger a pre-planned response capable of imposing severe and enduring costs upon the aggressor's economic and strategic infrastructure.
  • Assign senior military officers as observers and attachés to active conflict zones abroad.
  • Pursue, as a long-term objective, an independent nuclear deterrent capability.

Civil Defense System

  • Establish local civil-defense organizations responsible for emergency preparedness.
  • Expand disaster-response training among the population.
  • Develop continuity plans for municipalities, schools, hospitals, and critical infrastructure.

National Civic Service

  • Establish a period of national service for all citizens, regardless of gender.
  • Service pathways may include military service, civil protection, healthcare assistance, infrastructure projects, environmental restoration, or scientific research support.
  • Service assignments shall reflect national needs and individual aptitude.
III. Demographic Renewal

No civilization survives demographic collapse. Population decline is among the most serious threats to Greece.

Population Growth

  • Introduce a family solidarity contribution for childless adults above a specified age, excluding cases of infertility or medical incapacity.
  • Apply progressive rates based on income.
  • Gradually reduce the contribution for parents, with families of three or more children becoming net beneficiaries.

Family Support

  • Provide monthly state payments for every child until the age of eighteen.
  • Offer housing subsidies linked to family size and state-backed low-interest loans for first homes intended for family residence.
  • Prioritize family access to state-supported housing.
  • Guarantee free childcare services nationwide.
  • Extend paid parental leave for both mothers and fathers, with strong employment-protection provisions and adequate wage replacement.
  • Promote childbearing through national media campaigns emphasizing its civic significance.
  • Support research into advanced reproductive and genetic technologies.

Strategic Population Distribution

  • Encourage settlement in border regions, islands, and demographically declining areas with tax incentives, housing assistance, and infrastructure investment.
  • Integrate demographic policy with national development and territorial cohesion objectives.

Family Stability

  • Encourage long-term family stability through mediation, counseling, and family-support services.
  • Reduce unnecessary family breakdown while safeguarding the welfare of children.
  • Strengthen community institutions that support families across generations.
IV. Economic Development and Industrial Policy

Greece cannot rely indefinitely on tourism, services, and consumption. National strength requires production.

Strategic Economic Model

  • Reindustrialize the national economy through investment in pharmaceuticals, shipbuilding, semiconductors, defense industries, agricultural processing, and critical infrastructure.
  • Establish sovereign development banks and sovereign investment funds dedicated to acquiring strategic assets, technologies, infrastructure, and productive enterprises that strengthen national resilience and long-term development.
  • Maintain strategic state monopolies in critical sectors while preserving competition in consumer markets.
  • Expand the merchant fleet, modernize ports, and incentivize settlement on the islands.

Strategic Agricultural Policy

  • Designate food production as a strategic national priority.
  • Maintain minimum national self-sufficiency targets in key agricultural products.
  • Prohibit the sale of agricultural land to foreign individuals, foreign corporations, and foreign-controlled investment funds.
  • Establish state review mechanisms for transactions involving strategically significant agricultural properties.
  • Prioritize domestic production of seeds, fertilizers, and agricultural technologies.
  • Protect high-quality farmland from speculative development and urban expansion.
  • Promote climate-resilient agricultural practices and crop varieties.
  • The state may acquire strategically significant agricultural land and lease it through multi-generational cultivation contracts. Cultivators shall receive long-term security of tenure, low taxation, and protection from speculative land markets. In exchange, leaseholders shall maintain production targets and preserve the productive capacity of the land.

Maritime Economy

  • Maintain Greek leadership in commercial shipping and maritime services.
  • Expand domestic shipbuilding, ship repair, and maritime technology industries.
  • Promote sustainable fisheries, aquaculture, and marine-resource development.
  • Modernize ports and maritime logistics infrastructure.
  • Support marine biotechnology, pharmaceutical, and aquaculture research.
  • Develop offshore energy and maritime technology industries.
  • Encourage seabed resource mapping.
  • Promote the commercial utilization of marine resources consistent with long-term sustainability.

Strategic Tourism

  • Shift national tourism policy toward higher-value cultural, educational, medical, scientific, conference, and maritime tourism.
  • Reduce seasonal dependence through year-round tourism development.
  • Integrate tourism with cultural heritage, archaeological preservation, local production, and regional development.

Economic Sovereignty

  • Introduce a parallel currency at an appropriate diplomatic and economic moment to increase fiscal flexibility.
  • Enshrine constitutional debt limits.
  • Permit borrowing only for war, national emergencies, and strategic industrial projects.
  • Require long-term fiscal-impact assessments for major spending commitments.
  • Regularly evaluate pension and welfare systems according to demographic realities and long-term sustainability.

National Performance

  • Evaluate national economic policy according to its contribution to the long-term strength, resilience, and continuity of the Hellenic state rather than short-term economic growth alone.
  • Require the publication of an annual National Strength Report evaluating long-term trends in demographics, productivity, industrial capacity, scientific achievement, technological sovereignty, energy security, fiscal sustainability, military readiness, infrastructure quality, and other strategic indicators established by law.
  • Use long-term national performance indicators to guide strategic investment, infrastructure planning, scientific research, and industrial policy.

Innovation and Entrepreneurship

  • Reduce unnecessary administrative barriers to the creation and growth of new businesses.
  • Support technology commercialization and university spin-offs in strategically important sectors.
  • Expand venture-capital financing for productive and innovative enterprises.
  • Prioritize innovative domestic firms in public procurement where consistent with competition and value-for-money principles.
  • Strengthen protection of intellectual property generated through publicly funded research.

Anti-Offshoring Measures

  • Penalize the relocation of production abroad.
  • Favor domestic production in public procurement.
  • Prohibit strategic industries from relocating outside the country.

Tax Policy

  • Impose higher taxes on luxury consumption.
  • Increase taxation on gambling and games of chance.
  • Increase taxation on products associated with substantial public-health and social burdens.
  • Provide tax relief for strategic industries.
  • Adopt a tiered VAT system, reducing taxes on essential goods while increasing them on non-essential consumption.
  • Lower business taxation during the early stages of profitability.
  • Reduce fuel taxes for verified work-related commuters.

Labor Policy

  • Strengthen wage protections and enforcement of labor standards.
  • Enforce transparent recording of working hours and overtime.
  • Promote teleworking where appropriate.
  • Extend maternity and paternity leave.
  • Expand disability assistance and social integration programs.
  • Require worker councils and union participation mechanisms.

Market Competition and Economic Concentration

  • Prevent excessive concentration in strategically important sectors, including food distribution, energy, telecommunications, banking, and transportation.
  • Strengthen competition authorities and anti-cartel enforcement mechanisms.
  • Require periodic competition reviews of strategically important sectors where market concentration exceeds thresholds established by law.
  • Increase transparency in pricing practices throughout critical supply chains.
  • Support producer cooperatives and regional distribution networks capable of competing with dominant market actors.
  • Monitor excessive profit margins in sectors essential to household welfare.
  • Preserve market competition while preventing private monopolies and oligopolies from acquiring undue economic or political influence.

Market Transparency and Consumer Protection

  • Require clear origin and content labeling standards for food and consumer goods.
  • Increase penalties for fraud, deceptive practices, and corporate misconduct.
  • Strengthen consumer rights in digital and financial markets.

Housing Policy

  • Prioritize owner-occupation and family formation over financial speculation.
  • Expand construction of family-sized housing.
  • Provide preferential financing for first-home purchases by families.
  • Discourage large-scale speculative acquisition of residential property.
  • Establish public-private housing partnerships in strategically important regions.
V. Energy and Resource Security

Civilizations do not collapse only through invasion. They also collapse through dependence. Energy insecurity is strategic vulnerability in its purest form.

Energy Independence

  • Reassess and partially repeal environmental regulations deemed incompatible with energy security.
  • Expand lignite production.
  • Invest in synthetic fuel and oil-refinement technologies.
  • Reduce dependence on international energy markets.
  • Nationalize strategic energy corporations.
  • Maintain national ownership and strategic control of critical energy-transmission infrastructure.
  • Expand domestic electricity-generation capacity sufficient to maintain national resilience during major crises.
  • Strengthen national energy grids against cyber-attacks, sabotage, natural disasters, and wartime disruption.

Strategic Energy Diversification

  • Develop a diversified energy portfolio combining fossil fuels, renewable energy, energy storage, and emerging technologies.
  • Expand renewable-energy generation where it strengthens national self-sufficiency and grid resilience.
  • Prioritize energy-storage technologies capable of supporting intermittent power generation.
  • Encourage domestic production of renewable-energy components and related technologies.
  • Evaluate all energy investments according to long-term strategic value rather than ideological preference.

Offshore Resources and Maritime Energy

  • Defend Greece's sovereign rights to explore and develop offshore energy resources within its territorial waters and maritime zones.
  • Accelerate offshore geological surveying, seabed mapping, and exploration of maritime hydrocarbon and mineral deposits.
  • Develop domestic capabilities for offshore extraction, processing, and transportation of strategic resources.
  • Reduce dependence on foreign suppliers for critical maritime-energy infrastructure.

Water Security

  • Construct long-term reservoir infrastructure.
  • Implement continuous aquifer monitoring.
  • Expand desalination projects, particularly for island communities.

Climate Resilience

  • Prepare national infrastructure, settlements, and resource systems for long-term climatic change.
  • Expand drought-mitigation measures, water-storage capacity, and irrigation efficiency.
  • Strengthen coastal protection and adaptation planning for vulnerable regions.
  • Integrate climate-risk assessments into infrastructure, defense, agricultural, and demographic planning.

Strategic Minerals and Resource Security

  • Identify and secure domestic sources of critical minerals required for defense, energy, industrial, and technological production.
  • Expand geological surveying and resource mapping of mainland and island territories.
  • Prevent foreign control of strategically significant resource deposits.

National Strategic Reserves

  • Maintain strategic reserves of fuel, medicines, food, industrial inputs, rare earth materials, and critical technologies.
  • Regularly audit and rotate reserves to ensure readiness.
  • Gradually increase national holdings of gold and other strategic reserve assets.
  • Diversify reserve holdings to reduce vulnerability to external financial shocks.

Environmental Stewardship

  • Protect forests, agricultural land, water resources, and ecosystems as strategic national assets.
  • Expand reforestation initiatives.
  • Use drone surveillance to strengthen wildfire prevention during high-risk periods.
  • Enforce anti-pollution measures when environmental degradation threatens agriculture, tourism, or critical infrastructure.
  • Establish monitoring programs for invasive terrestrial and marine species. Support research and commercial utilization of invasive species where economically viable.
  • Develop rapid-response mechanisms to protect agriculture, fisheries, ecosystems, and public health.
VI. Science, Research, and Artificial Intelligence

Scientific and technological leadership will determine the global balance of power in the twenty-first century.

Research Policy

  • Align scientific investment with long-term civilizational challenges, including demographic renewal, public health, energy security, food production, maritime development, water resilience, and national defense.
  • Reduce dependence on foreign technologies in strategically critical sectors.
  • Preserve public ownership of universities and maintain tuition-free access.
  • Permit private funding while safeguarding public oversight.
  • Introduce transparent university rankings to guide scholarship allocation.
  • Establish special research jurisdictions dedicated to high-priority scientific fields.
  • Permit research prohibited or excessively restricted in other countries, subject to transparent oversight and national-interest review.
  • Accelerate approval procedures for strategic scientific projects.
  • Provide competitive funding for high-performing researchers in strategically important fields.
  • Support venture-capital formation and technology-commercialization mechanisms in strategically important sectors.
  • Prioritize the domestic commercialization and strategic application of publicly funded scientific research.

Strategic Research Priorities

  • Biotechnology, including cancer treatment, genetic diseases, longevity, fertility research, food security, drought resistance, crop productivity, and defense applications.
  • Maritime robotics, autonomous vessels, maritime surveillance systems, aquaculture innovation, offshore energy systems, marine biotechnology, and climate-adaptation technologies for coastal regions.
  • Water technologies, including desalination, water recycling, aquifer management, and drought mitigation.
  • Defense technologies, including autonomous systems, cyber-defense, electronic warfare, resilient communications, and strategic deterrence capabilities.
  • Complexity science, systems analysis, forecasting, and strategic planning.

Digital Sovereignty

  • Reduce dependence on foreign software platforms and critical digital infrastructure.
  • Expand domestic capabilities in cybersecurity, cloud infrastructure, secure communications, and strategic software development.
  • Protect critical national digital systems against cyber-attacks, espionage, and foreign interference.
  • Preserve national control over strategically important public data.

    • Artificial Intelligence

    • Establish a National AI Strategy Committee.
    • Deploy AI-assisted systems in public administration for fraud detection, budget optimization, forecasting, and anti-corruption efforts.
    • Develop Greek-language AI models.
    • Create an AI Adjustment Fund to support workers displaced by automation.
    • Explore the integration of AI into military command structures.

    Space and Strategic Technologies

    • Develop national capabilities in satellite technologies, remote sensing, geospatial intelligence, and space-based communications.
    • Pursue independent capabilities in strategically important space technologies while participating in international space programs where consistent with national interests.
    • Prepare the Hellenic state for participation in future space exploration, extraterrestrial settlement, and emerging domains of human activity beyond Earth.
    • Preserve the sovereign right of future generations to defend Hellenic interests in any domain of human expansion.
    VII. Education

    Education determines the quality of future citizens, administrators, scientists, and leaders. A civilization cannot outperform the institutions that train its people.

    Educational Structure

    • Introduce continuous national examinations beginning in primary education.
    • Advancement shall reflect demonstrated competence and achievement rather than automatic progression.
    • Implement early identification of aptitude, discipline, and potential.
    • Create differentiated educational tracks aligned with talent and competence.
    • Students may move between educational tracks through demonstrated performance and achievement.
    • Provide accelerated educational pathways and specialized institutions for exceptionally high-performing students.

    Curriculum

    • Expand civic duty and national consciousness education.
    • Introduce practical life-skills courses.
    • Cultivate discipline, responsibility, and long-term thinking.
    • Develop advanced analytical and strategic reasoning within specialized academic tracks.
    • Align school curricula and examinations to eliminate dependence on private tutoring.
    • Regularly review curricula to ensure relevance, rigor, and national priorities.

    Vocational and Technical Education

    • Expand technical schools aligned with national industrial priorities.
    • Strengthen vocational pathways in manufacturing, agriculture, shipbuilding, energy, healthcare, and information technology.
    • Promote apprenticeships and partnerships between educational institutions and industry.
    • Improve the quality, prestige, and economic value of vocational education.

    Teaching Profession

    • Provide incentives for educators serving in remote regions.
    • Implement permanent performance evaluations.
    • Evaluate educational institutions according to measurable student outcomes.
    • Reward outstanding teaching performance and educational outcomes.
    VIII. Healthcare and Social Welfare

    The state exists not only to defend borders, but to preserve the physical and social health of its people.

    Healthcare Reform

    • Guarantee free preventive healthcare services.
    • Fully digitalize medical records.
    • Introduce mandatory annual health screenings.
    • Expand rural healthcare access.
    • Evaluate hospital administrators according to measurable outcomes.
    • Increase public mental-health capacity.
    • Improve incentives for nurses through higher pay and earlier retirement options.
    • Expand infrastructure for elderly care.

    Strategic Health Security

    • Expand domestic pharmaceutical and medical-equipment production.
    • Maintain strategic reserves of critical medicines, vaccines, medical supplies, and emergency equipment.
    • Strengthen preparedness for pandemics, biological threats, and mass-casualty emergencies.
    • Establish measurable national targets for life expectancy, fertility, infant mortality, obesity, preventable diseases, and healthy life-years.

    National Vitality and Longevity

    • Promote physical fitness, healthy nutrition, and preventive medicine through national initiatives.
    • Expand access to public sports facilities, walking routes, and recreational infrastructure.
    • Reduce the long-term burden of chronic diseases through early intervention and health education.
    • Encourage active and productive aging through community programs and preventive healthcare.
    • Integrate public-health objectives into education, urban planning, workplace policy, and demographic strategy.
    • Support research into longevity, fertility, age-related diseases, and population health.
    IX. Infrastructure and Transportation

    Roads, transport systems, and urban planning shape daily life more than most legislation. Functional infrastructure is civilization made visible.

    Infrastructure Governance

    • Require public disclosure of all major public-works contracts.
    • Establish strict completion deadlines and performance penalties for contractors.
    • Publish real-time progress tracking for major infrastructure projects.
    • Prohibit repeated contract renegotiations except under extraordinary circumstances.
    • Require independent post-completion audits for major infrastructure projects.

    Strategic Infrastructure

    • Develop long-term national infrastructure plans extending beyond electoral cycles.
    • Expand strategic energy, water, transportation, and communications infrastructure.
    • Strengthen infrastructure resilience against war, cyber-attacks, natural disasters, and supply-chain disruptions.
    • Construct redundant infrastructure systems for critical national functions.
    • Expand domestic data-center capacity and secure national digital infrastructure.

    Mobility and Urban Planning

    • Expand parking infrastructure, including underground facilities.
    • Develop a nationwide real-time traffic monitoring and guidance system.
    • Increase investment in public transportation quality and capacity.
    • Address national transport bottlenecks through targeted road construction.
    • Modernize freight and logistics corridors connecting ports, industrial zones, and border regions.
    • Promote urban planning that balances density, quality of life, and long-term sustainability.

    National Connectivity

    • Expand high-speed digital connectivity throughout the country.
    • Improve transportation links between major cities, border regions, and islands.
    • Eliminate infrastructure disparities between urban, rural, and island regions.
    X. Immigration and Citizenship

    Borders define the boundaries of political community. Immigration policy must serve the long-term interests of the nation, not short-term ideological preferences.

    Border Security

    • Prioritize the prevention of unlawful entry over post-entry administrative processing.
    • Maintain effective control of national land and maritime borders through surveillance systems, physical infrastructure, maritime patrols, and rapid-response capabilities.
    • Expand national capacity for the identification, processing, detention, and removal of unlawful entrants.
    • Accelerate deportation procedures for individuals lacking a legal right to remain within the country.
    • Strengthen action against human-smuggling networks and unlawful migration routes.
    • Reduce incentives for unlawful immigration through strict enforcement of employment, residency, and documentation requirements.

    Refugee Policy

    • Prioritize eventual voluntary or assisted repatriation whenever conditions permit.
    • Concentrate assistance on basic necessities, healthcare, and security rather than long-term welfare dependency.
    • Ensure refugee assistance does not exceed the economic position of low-income Greek citizens.
    • Locate refugee accommodation according to national security, demographic, and administrative considerations.
    • Distinguish clearly between temporary refuge and permanent settlement.

    Legal Immigration

    • Permit legal immigration only where it serves clearly identified national economic, demographic, scientific, or strategic interests.
    • Require employers to demonstrate inability to recruit sufficient domestic labor before obtaining foreign-worker permits.
    • Ensure foreign labor does not undermine domestic wages, working conditions, or employment opportunities.
    • Require foreign workers to maintain private health insurance and contribute to national social-insurance systems.
    • Require legal immigrants to demonstrate respect for Greek laws and institutions.
    • Require demonstrated economic self-sufficiency for long-term residence.
    • Establish a Civic Residency Contribution for non-citizen permanent residents in recognition of the benefits and protections provided by the Hellenic state.
    • Reserve political authority and constitutional privileges primarily for citizens.

    Citizenship and Civic Status

    • Replace the binary citizenship model with multiple tiers of civic status reflecting differing degrees of political participation, rights, obligations, and integration.
    • Full civic status shall remain linked to pure intergenerational membership within the Hellenic community.
    • Ownership of land and strategically significant real estate shall be reserved to civic-status categories designated by law.
    • All civic-status categories may lease residential or commercial property subject to conditions established by law.
    • Marriage shall not create any automatic entitlement to civic-status advancement.
    • Exceptions to ordinary civic-status rules shall be permitted only under exceptional circumstances and according to criteria established by constitutional law.
    • Pursue renegotiation of relevant European agreements where necessary.

    Assimilation and Cultural Integration

    • Prioritize the preservation of the historical, cultural, and demographic continuity of the Hellenic nation.
    • Encourage cultural assimilation into the Hellenic national community while maintaining public order and social cohesion.
    • Reject the formation of parallel societies and permanently segregated communities.
    XI. Culture, Identity, and Religion

    Material prosperity alone cannot sustain a civilization. Nations endure through culture, memory, identity, and purpose.

    Civilizational Identity

    • Promote awareness of Hellenic history as a continuous civilizational tradition linking Classical Greece, Byzantium, the modern Greek state, and the Fourth Hellenic Civilization.
    • Strengthen historical consciousness through education, museums, public commemorations, media, and cultural institutions.
    • Encourage citizens to view family formation, public service, military service, scientific achievement, and civic responsibility as contributions to a shared civilizational mission.
    • Preserve and develop national symbols, ceremonies, and traditions that reinforce social cohesion and historical continuity.
    • Preserve, enrich, and promote the Greek language as a foundational pillar of Hellenic civilization.

    Cultural Policy

    • Support a domestic film industry that strengthens national identity and explores lesser-known chapters of Hellenic history.
    • Promote local traditions, customs, and regional cultural heritage through national media and educational initiatives.
    • Encourage volunteer organizations, local associations, charitable initiatives, and community institutions that strengthen civic responsibility and social cohesion.
    • Organize international competitions in Ancient Greek language, philosophy, history, and classical studies.
    • Provide support for artists, architects, musicians, writers, and scholars producing original work rooted in Hellenic traditions.
    • Encourage alternatives to consumerism, cultural homogenization, and excessive dependence on globalized mass culture through public cultural initiatives.
    • Support the preservation and development of traditional crafts, architecture, and clothing.

    International Cultural Influence

    • Promote Hellenic culture, philosophy, language, and historical achievements through cultural diplomacy and international educational programs.
    • Support creators, researchers, educators, and public figures who strengthen Greece's global cultural presence.
    • Establish international centers dedicated to the study of Hellenic civilization.
    • Expand Greek-language education abroad and strengthen ties with philhellenic communities worldwide.

    Media and Information

    • Strengthen resilience against foreign information warfare, influence operations, and coordinated disinformation campaigns.
    • Increase transparency regarding foreign funding and ownership of media organizations.
    • Encourage the development of independent Greek digital platforms, media organizations, and content-production capabilities.

    Religious Policy

    • Restrict official places of worship to faiths regarded as integral to Greek identity: Orthodoxy and the Ancient Hellenic religion.
    • Establish a network of ecclesiastical representatives attached to Greek diplomatic missions in Orthodox-majority countries.
    • Establish church-supported restoration and preservation programs for monasteries, churches, manuscripts, temples, and sacred sites.
    • Encourage religious institutions to participate in elderly care, charitable assistance, disaster relief, and community support.
