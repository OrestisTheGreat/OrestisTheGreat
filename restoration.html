National Restoration Program

A Hundred-Year Plan Toward the Fourth Hellenic Civilization

The Restoration Program is the strategic blueprint of Orestis II for the long-term renewal of Greece.

Its objective is not merely economic recovery, but the reconstruction of state capacity, demographic vitality, technological strength, strategic sovereignty, and civilizational confidence.

I. Governance II. Sovereignty III. Demographics IV. Economy V. Energy VI. Science & AI VII. Education VIII. Healthcare IX. Infrastructure X. Citizenship XI. Culture

Executive Summary

Greece is trapped in a system of chronic institutional failure: short-term parliamentary politics, entrenched clientelism, demographic collapse, economic dependency, and strategic vulnerability.

These are not temporary problems but structural failures of governance that have steadily weakened national power and cohesion.

The National Restoration Program proposes a full-scale transformation of the Greek state and society.

I. Institutional Redesign

Replace short-term political management with a high-capacity strategic state. Key national domains are removed from electoral cycles and placed under permanent constitutional and technocratic institutions. Meritocracy, strict anti-corruption enforcement, long-term forecasting, and institutional continuity replace clientelist governance.

II. Strategic Sovereignty

Restore full national autonomy across defense, energy, food, finance, and critical infrastructure. Greece develops independent military capacity, domestic production, secure supply chains, strategic reserves, and maritime dominance to ensure survival under global instability.

III. Demographic & Productive Renewal

Reverse population decline and rebuild productive capacity. Pro-family policy, large-scale housing support, strategic settlement of border and island regions, and a shift from tourism-dependent consumption toward industrialization, agriculture, advanced technology, AI, and manufacturing.

IV. Civilizational Renewal

Rebuild national cohesion through education, cultural revival, historical continuity, and strong civic identity. Immigration, citizenship, and integration policy are structured around long-term cultural and demographic stability, alongside strict border control and tiered civic status.

Long-Term Goal

Transform Greece into a sovereign, resilient, highly productive, demographically stable, and technologically advanced civilization capable of long-term historical continuity.

I. Constitutional and Institutional Reform

The greatest weakness of modern Greece is institutional rather than civilizational. The state remains excessively reactive, bureaucratic, and constrained by short electoral cycles.

Restoration begins with the creation of institutions capable of long-term strategic governance.

Constitutional Order

  • Establish a mixed constitutional system combining democratic participation, technocratic administration, constitutional continuity, and long-term strategic governance.
  • Long-term objective: the creation of a modern Res Publica model balancing popular sovereignty, institutional competence, and civilizational continuity.
  • Adopt a doctrine of National Historical Mission, whereby the state pursues objectives extending across decades and centuries rather than electoral cycles.
  • Where national objectives conflict, institutions shall prioritize the long-term continuity, sovereignty, security, and civilizational development of the Hellenic nation over short-term economic or political considerations.
  • Create Strategic Constitutional Authorities responsible for safeguarding long-term national priorities, including demographics, energy, artificial intelligence, defense, water security, and other domains designated by constitutional law.
  • Select members of the Strategic Constitutional Authorities through national examinations, integrity reviews, and mission-alignment assessments. Require approval by the Constitutional Monarch. Provide long, staggered terms to ensure continuity and independence.
  • Within their constitutionally defined jurisdictions, Strategic Constitutional Authorities shall possess authority superior to ordinary legislation and executive directives. Their mandate shall be to preserve and advance the long-term interests of the Fourth Hellenic Civilization.
  • The mission, jurisdiction, and constitutional powers of the Strategic Constitutional Authorities may be amended only through extraordinary constitutional procedures requiring a supermajority of the national legislature.
  • Introduce permanent online referendums within clearly defined constitutional limits. Citizens may propose and approve legislation directly in areas not reserved to the Strategic Constitutional Authorities.
  • Ultimate sovereignty resides in the Hellenic people. Democratic institutions, strategic institutions, and constitutional continuity shall function as complementary sources of legitimacy.
  • The Constitutional Monarch serves as guardian of constitutional continuity, national unity, and emergency state authority. During war, constitutional breakdown, or the inability to form a government, emergency executive authority may temporarily pass to the Crown under conditions established by constitutional law.
  • The reigning Monarch may nominate a successor. Such nomination shall require approval by both the national legislature and the Strategic Constitutional Authorities before taking effect.

State Administration

  • Ministers and senior ministry officials shall be selected through competitive national examinations rather than partisan appointment.
  • Preparation materials, examination standards, and evaluation criteria shall be publicly available.
  • Any citizen possessing full civic status may participate in the relevant examinations.
  • The role of ministers is administrative rather than political. Ministers shall not determine national policy but shall execute constitutional objectives, legislation, and government directives with maximum efficiency and competence.
  • The elected government shall retain responsibility for legislation, political direction, and the establishment of measurable national objectives.
  • Each ministry shall operate according to performance targets established by law or government policy.
  • Officials who consistently fail to achieve established objectives shall be removed from office.
  • Promotion and advancement shall be based on measurable outcomes, demonstrated competence, and public service performance rather than tenure, personal connections, or partisan affiliation.

Anti-Corruption and Public Accountability

  • Establish an independent auditing authority responsible for monitoring politicians, judges, and ministers.
  • Select members through Venetian-style sortition among qualified citizens with no active political affiliations.
  • Create a second auditing body to oversee and monitor the first.
  • Implement continuous oversight of financial transactions involving elected officials and senior public office holders.

Strategic Analysis

  • Establish a National Forecasting Office responsible for long-term demographic, economic, technological, and geopolitical analysis.
  • Require periodic evaluation of major public policies according to measurable national objectives.
  • Expand government data collection while protecting information security.
  • Require strategic scenario planning for major national risks, including war, demographic decline, technological disruption, climate change, financial crises, and supply-chain disruptions.

Judicial Reform

  • Appoint judges through impartial Venetian-style selection procedures designed to maximize independence.
  • Accelerate legal proceedings through strict procedural deadlines.
  • Fully digitalize judicial administration, including submissions and case management.
  • Require transitional periods and stability guarantees for legislation affecting taxation, business licensing, and investment.
  • Conduct periodic reviews to eliminate obsolete laws and regulations.
  • Require regulatory-impact assessments for major legislation.
  • Limit parliamentary immunity to acts directly connected with legislative functions.
  • Establish specialized commercial, investment, and technology courts for disputes involving productive enterprise, intellectual property, infrastructure, and strategic industries.
  • Prioritize rapid adjudication of disputes involving productive investment, critical infrastructure, strategic industries, and public procurement.
  • Require insolvency and restructuring procedures to prioritize, where economically viable, the preservation of productive enterprises, employment, technological capabilities, and strategically important industrial assets over liquidation.

Public Order and Internal Security

  • Public safety shall be evaluated according to measurable reductions in violent crime, organized crime, response times, case clearance rates, and public perceptions of safety. Police leadership shall be held accountable for achieving these objectives.
  • Expand the deployment of public security cameras in high-risk urban areas to deter crime and improve investigative capacity.
  • Integrate surveillance systems under a strict legal framework governing data retention, access control, and judicial oversight.
  • Increase lighting, maintenance, sanitation, and visible policing in high-crime neighborhoods.
  • Strengthen measures against organized crime, trafficking networks, corruption, and repeat violent offenders.
  • Reduce open-air drug markets through sustained policing and social intervention.
  • Coordinate policing, municipal services, and urban renewal in districts experiencing persistent disorder.
  • Introduce citizen whistleblower incentives for reporting serious unlawful activity.
  • Prevent the occupation, vandalism, or unlawful disruption of educational and public facilities.
  • Eliminate illegal settlements through lawful procedures.
  • Enforce building, sanitation, and property regulations equally.
  • Establish permanent security capabilities for university campuses under clearly defined legal authority.
  • Suppress hooligan violence through stricter penalties, enhanced event security, and stronger accountability for sports organizations.
  • Counter political violence, extremist violence, and organized attempts to undermine constitutional order.
  • Distinguish rehabilitation pathways for low-risk offenders while maintaining severe penalties for violent and organized criminal activity.

Local Governance

  • Elect local officials through a combination of public vote and competency assessment.
  • Expand local authority over matters of regional concern.
  • Subject local governments to oversight by an independent auditing branch.
  • Establish binding local referendums for matters falling within municipal authority, subject to constitutional safeguards.
  • Expand the fiscal autonomy of municipalities by assigning clearly defined local revenue sources together with corresponding responsibilities.
  • Strengthen the administrative and technical capacity of municipalities to plan, procure, and manage infrastructure projects.
  • Publish all municipal contracts, expenditures, procurement decisions, and council votes through standardized digital platforms.
  • Extend the unified digital-government platform to all municipalities through common digital standards, shared infrastructure, and interoperable services.
  • Require public consultation for major municipal development projects.
  • Municipal self-government shall operate within the constitutional framework established for the Hellenic state. National strategic matters reserved by the Constitution shall remain outside municipal jurisdiction.

Administrative Modernization

  • Create a unified citizen account for all government services.
  • Eliminate paper-based certificates exchanged between public agencies.
  • Impose strict service-delivery deadlines throughout the public administration.
  • Simplify legislation and administrative procedures through plain-language drafting and standardized processes.
  • Reduce administrative infrastructure costs through digital services and remote work where appropriate.
  • Utilize teleworking to support regional development and population distribution objectives.
II. National Strategy and Sovereignty

A state that cannot defend its interests cannot secure its future. Greece must regain strategic autonomy while remaining realistic about geopolitical constraints.

Foreign Policy

  • Greece shall maintain relations with all major powers while preserving maximum strategic independence.
  • The Constitutional Monarch shall serve as the permanent representative of the Hellenic State in matters of constitutional continuity and state ceremony
  • Maintain short- and medium-term alignment with the European Union and NATO while gradually increasing strategic autonomy.
  • Develop strategic partnerships with European states that support greater national sovereignty and institutional flexibility within the European framework.
  • Renegotiate or withdraw from selected European treaties, particularly in immigration and environmental policy.
  • Expand strategic intelligence, counter-intelligence, and influence capabilities in support of national security and foreign policy objectives.
  • Pursue long-term alignment with rising non-Western powers, including China.
  • Extend Greek territorial waters to twelve nautical miles regardless of external opposition.
  • Prepare for the possibility of future military confrontation with Turkey.
  • Revive the Megali Idea as a strategic and cultural counterweight to Turkish expansionism.

Hellenic Diaspora Strategy

  • Encourage the return of highly skilled members of the diaspora.
  • Support Greek-language and cultural education among diaspora communities.
  • Encourage voluntary settlement, investment, and economic activity by Greeks in historically Hellenic regions.

Maritime Strategy

  • Strengthen the protection and monitoring of territorial waters, exclusive economic zones, and critical maritime infrastructure.
  • Expand maritime domain awareness through naval, aerial, space-based, and digital surveillance capabilities.
  • Develop a domestic naval-industrial sector capable of designing, constructing, maintaining, and modernizing warships and autonomous maritime systems.
  • Prioritize technologies that strengthen maritime denial, coastal defense, and island security.
  • Reduce long-term dependence on foreign suppliers for critical naval capabilities.
  • Maintain control of critical sea lines of communication and island approaches.
  • Deny hostile forces freedom of action within the Aegean and Eastern Mediterranean.
  • Integrate naval, air, missile, cyber, and unmanned systems into a unified maritime-defense network.
  • Emphasize submarines, missiles, drones, autonomous systems, and distributed naval power. Avoid dependence on expensive power-projection platforms unsuited to Greek geography.

Island Development Strategy

  • Establish a doctrine of Island Continuity whereby the maintenance of permanent habitation on strategically significant islands constitutes a national-security and territorial-cohesion objective.
  • Establish long-term demographic objectives for frontier and strategically important islands and periodically evaluate progress toward those objectives.
  • Prioritize energy independence, water security, healthcare access, digital connectivity, and resilient public services for island communities.
  • Encourage permanent settlement, family formation, and productive economic activity in sparsely populated and strategically important islands through coordinated demographic, housing, and infrastructure policies.
  • Strengthen transportation links between island regions and the mainland through reliable maritime and air connections designed to ensure continuous access to essential public services, healthcare, education, and economic opportunity.
  • Develop integrated regional island networks centered on strategically designated hub islands providing advanced healthcare, higher education, specialized public administration, logistics, and commercial services for surrounding island communities.
  • Expand strategic infrastructure capable of supporting both civilian and defense requirements, including ports, airfields, communications, energy systems, water infrastructure, emergency services, and logistics facilities.
  • Develop selected islands as centers for maritime research, aquaculture, renewable energy, desalination technologies, marine biotechnology, advanced logistics, autonomous maritime systems, and other strategically important maritime industries.
  • Promote remote work, digital entrepreneurship, and knowledge-based economic activity through universal high-speed digital infrastructure and advanced telecommunications.
  • Provide long-term housing solutions and service incentives sufficient to recruit and retain healthcare professionals, educators, emergency personnel, military personnel, and other strategically important public servants in island communities.
  • Integrate island development into national defense, maritime strategy, demographic planning, scientific research, and economic development through long-term regional development plans tailored to the strategic characteristics of each island cluster.
  • Evaluate tourism development according to the long-term carrying capacity of each island.
  • Strengthen local value creation by integrating tourism with agriculture, fisheries, aquaculture, maritime industries, food processing, traditional crafts, cultural heritage, scientific research, and regional entrepreneurship.
  • Promote the long-term preservation of the architectural character, cultural heritage, and environmental quality of island communities while supporting appropriate modernization and climate resilience.

Defense Reform

  • Greece shall maintain military capabilities sufficient to defend national sovereignty, deter aggression, protect Hellenic interests, and exploit favorable strategic opportunities when historical circumstances permit.
  • Prioritize domestic defense production and self-sufficiency.
  • Emphasize drones, autonomous systems, and cost-effective weapon platforms.
  • Ensure the continued functioning of the state during war through redundant energy systems, dispersed industrial capacity, strategic reserves, secure communications, cyber-defense capabilities, and continuity-of-government planning.
  • Develop a strategic destruction doctrine: any attack upon Greece shall trigger a pre-planned response capable of imposing severe and enduring costs upon the aggressor's economic and strategic infrastructure.
  • Assign senior military officers as observers and attachés to active conflict zones abroad.
  • Pursue, as a long-term objective, an independent nuclear deterrent capability.

Civil Defense System

  • Establish local civil-defense organizations responsible for emergency preparedness.
  • Expand disaster-response training among the population.
  • Develop continuity plans for municipalities, schools, hospitals, and critical infrastructure.

National Civic Service

  • Establish a period of national service for all citizens, regardless of gender.
  • Service pathways may include military service, civil protection, healthcare assistance, infrastructure projects, environmental restoration, or scientific research support.
  • Service assignments shall reflect national needs and individual aptitude.
Strategic Principle:

“Enrich the Country, Strengthen the Military” - Fukoku Kyōhei (Meiji Japan)
III. Demographic Renewal

No civilization survives demographic collapse. Population decline is among the most serious threats to Greece.

Population Growth

  • Introduce a family solidarity contribution for childless adults above a specified age, excluding cases of infertility or medical incapacity.
  • Apply progressive rates based on income.
  • Gradually reduce the contribution for parents, with families of three or more children becoming net beneficiaries.

Family Formation and Stability

  • Provide monthly state payments for every child until the age of eighteen.
  • Offer housing subsidies linked to family size and state-backed low-interest loans for first homes intended for family residence.
  • Prioritize family access to state-supported housing.
  • Guarantee free childcare services nationwide.
  • Extend paid parental leave for both mothers and fathers, with strong employment-protection provisions and adequate wage replacement.
  • Promote childbearing through national media campaigns emphasizing its civic significance.
  • Encourage long-term family stability through mediation, counseling, and family-support services.
  • Reduce unnecessary family breakdown while safeguarding the welfare of children.
  • Strengthen community institutions that support families across generations.
  • Support research into advanced reproductive and genetic technologies.

Strategic Population Distribution

  • Encourage settlement in border regions, islands, and demographically declining areas with tax incentives, housing assistance, and infrastructure investment.
  • Integrate demographic policy with national development and territorial cohesion objectives.
IV. Economic Development and Industrial Policy

Greece cannot rely indefinitely on tourism, services, and consumption. National strength requires production.

Strategic Economic Model

  • The principal objective of economic policy shall be to increase the productive, technological, scientific, and industrial capabilities of the nation across successive generations.
  • The Hellenic economy shall combine competitive markets with strategic state direction. Competitive enterprise, private property, entrepreneurship, and market exchange shall constitute the primary mechanisms of economic organization except in sectors designated by the Strategic Constitutional Authorities as essential to national sovereignty, long-term resilience, or constitutional continuity.
  • National economic policy shall prioritize productive investment over speculative finance, technological advancement over resource dependence, and long-term productive capacity over short-term consumption.
  • National economic policy shall pursue full productive employment through industrial development, technological progress, infrastructure investment, regional development, and support for productive enterprise.
  • Reindustrialize the national economy through the development of strategically important manufacturing sectors, including pharmaceuticals, defense industries, shipbuilding, semiconductors, advanced machinery, chemicals, agricultural processing, and other industries designated by the Strategic Constitutional Authorities.
  • Strengthen domestic value chains to increase national productive self-sufficiency and reduce dependence on imported strategic goods.
  • Establish sovereign development banks and sovereign investment funds dedicated to financing strategic infrastructure, industrial modernization, technological innovation, and nationally significant productive enterprises.
  • Establish a National Investment and Industrial Development Authority responsible for identifying strategically important investments, coordinating industrial development, attracting productive foreign investment, and supporting the long-term modernization of the national economy.
  • Develop industrial clusters linking manufacturers, suppliers, universities, research institutions, financial institutions, and regional governments.
  • Develop long-term regional industrial strategies based on the comparative advantages, natural resources, research capacity, workforce characteristics, and infrastructure of each region.
  • Where economically viable, support the restructuring and productive recovery of strategically important enterprises.

National Performance

  • Evaluate national economic policy according to its contribution to the long-term strength, resilience, and continuity of the Hellenic state rather than short-term economic growth alone.
  • Require the publication of an annual National Strength Report evaluating long-term trends in demographics, productivity, industrial capacity, scientific achievement, technological sovereignty, energy security, fiscal sustainability, military readiness, infrastructure quality, and other strategic indicators established by law.
  • Use long-term national performance indicators to guide strategic investment, infrastructure planning, scientific research, and industrial policy.
  • All national strategic indicators shall be coordinated through the National Performance Framework.

Strategic Agricultural Policy

  • Designate food production as a core domain of national sovereignty and long-term civilizational security, equivalent in strategic importance to energy, defense, and demographic stability.
  • Establish minimum national self-sufficiency thresholds in strategically critical food categories, regularly reviewed according to demographic needs, climate conditions, and geopolitical risk assessments.
  • Prohibit the sale of agricultural land to foreign states, foreign-controlled entities, and non-sovereign investment structures. Establish a national screening mechanism for all transactions involving agricultural land, water resources, and strategically significant rural assets, based on long-term security and productive capacity criteria.
  • Prioritize domestic production of seeds, fertilizers, and agricultural technologies.
  • Develop a structured national agricultural system based on integrated producer organizations, cooperative networks, and contract-based production frameworks that strengthen bargaining power, reduce fragmentation, and stabilize supply chains.
  • Establish agricultural credit and investment institutions aligned with long-term productive capacity rather than short-term financial return, including specialized development finance mechanisms for rural production systems and modernization.
  • Promote contract farming models that align production with national demand forecasting, export strategy, and industrial processing capacity.
  • Prioritize the expansion of domestic agro-industrial capacity, emphasizing food processing, preservation technologies, packaging, quality certification, and high-value transformation of primary agricultural output into export-grade products.
  • Promote integrated pest management, biological crop protection, and evidence-based disease-control systems that reduce chemical inputs while maintaining productivity and export-quality standards.
  • Promote research, commercialization, and export of high-value food technologies and specialized products, including functional foods, medical nutrition, sports nutrition, infant nutrition, nutraceuticals, and advanced food ingredients.
  • Strengthen vertical integration across the food system, linking producers, processors, logistics networks, and export channels into coordinated national value chains.
  • Reduce post-harvest losses through modern storage systems, cold-chain infrastructure, digital logistics, improved packaging technologies, and advanced inventory management across agricultural supply chains.
  • Introduce a unified Hellenic Agri-Food Identity Framework combining certification systems (PDO/PGI/organic standards), traceability infrastructure, and coordinated international branding strategy to enhance global competitiveness.
  • Establish a National Agricultural Innovation Network within the National Research System, integrating universities, research institutes, applied technology centers, agricultural enterprises, and producer organizations into a unified framework for agricultural modernization.
  • Establish a nationwide agricultural extension system that accelerates the transfer of scientific knowledge, modern production methods, biological crop protection, precision agriculture, digital technologies, certification systems, and farm modernization practices from research institutions to producers.
  • Prioritize development and diffusion of precision agriculture systems, including satellite monitoring, AI-driven forecasting, sensor-based irrigation, drone-assisted cultivation, and automated farm management technologies.
  • Support participation of domestic firms and research institutions in international research programs and technology partnerships, ensuring structured transfer of knowledge and commercialization of innovation.
  • Protect high-quality agricultural land from speculative conversion and urban encroachment through long-term zoning and productivity protection frameworks.
  • Establish a national agricultural land registry linked to soil quality, water availability, and climate suitability data to guide long-term cultivation strategy and optimize crop allocation.
  • Develop long-term regional cultivation strategies identifying the most suitable existing and emerging crops for each region according to soil characteristics, water availability, climate projections, market demand, infrastructure, and comparative advantage.
  • Promote high-efficiency agricultural systems, including controlled-environment agriculture, greenhouse clusters, and high-value intensive cultivation models adapted to regional climatic conditions.
  • Support diversified rural economic structures combining agriculture, agro-processing, tourism, and digital remote work integration to strengthen demographic resilience in rural regions.
  • Establish an Agricultural and Agri-Industrial Service within the Ministry of Foreign Affairs to coordinate export expansion, market intelligence, and international trade facilitation through Greek diplomatic missions.
  • Expand agricultural market intelligence and long-term demand forecasting to identify emerging export opportunities and strategic supply vulnerabilities.
  • Expand strategic export markets through coordinated branding, quality certification alignment, and long-term bilateral agricultural trade frameworks.
  • Defend and expand international legal protection for Hellenic protected geographical indications and other nationally significant agricultural brands through trade negotiations, diplomatic engagement, and coordinated legal enforcement.
  • Prioritize the transformation of agricultural exports from raw commodity dependence toward branded, high-value, and technologically certified product categories.
  • Establish a National Food System Stability Index to monitor affordability, supply resilience, production capacity, and external dependency risks across essential food categories.
  • Introduce structured public procurement mechanisms prioritizing domestically produced essential food goods for public institutions, including education, healthcare, and defense systems, ensuring stable baseline demand for national producers.
  • Develop strategic stabilization mechanisms for essential food commodities during periods of external price volatility, including risk buffering systems and supply shock mitigation instruments.
  • Strengthen structured education pipelines between agricultural universities, vocational institutions, and productive enterprises to ensure continuous diffusion of innovation and technical competence.
  • Promote on-farm energy generation, energy storage, and renewable-energy systems where they reduce production costs, strengthen energy resilience, and improve long-term agricultural competitiveness.
  • Promote climate-resilient agricultural systems designed for long-term ecological stability under changing climatic conditions.
  • Support water-efficient irrigation systems, soil regeneration practices, carbon-conscious cultivation methods, and adaptive crop selection aligned with national resource constraints.
  • Integrate agricultural planning with national water security, energy systems, and environmental resilience strategy.
  • Prioritize agricultural production systems compatible with long-term national water availability, promoting crop selection, irrigation technologies, and regional cultivation patterns that maximize productivity per unit of water consumed.
  • Develop national agricultural risk-management systems combining insurance mechanisms, climate forecasting, disaster preparedness, forward contracting, voluntary price-risk management instruments, and rapid recovery support to preserve long-term productive capacity.
  • Preserve indigenous crop varieties, livestock breeds, seed banks, and agricultural genetic resources as strategic national assets supporting long-term food security, climate adaptation, and scientific research.
  • The state may acquire strategically significant agricultural land and allocate it through long-term, including multi-generational, productive lease structures. Leaseholders shall receive security of tenure, preferential access to credit, low taxation, and protection from speculative land markets in exchange for maintaining production standards, ecological stewardship, and the productive capacity of the land.

National Livestock Strategy

  • Treat livestock production as a strategic component of national food security and agricultural resilience. Maintain domestic productive capacity in meat, dairy, and other essential livestock sectors sufficient to reduce excessive dependence on external supply during periods of international disruption.
  • Protect, restore, and scientifically manage national pasturelands to support sustainable livestock production, biodiversity, soil conservation, and long-term rural productivity.
  • Implement scientific grazing management systems that balance livestock production with ecosystem regeneration, wildfire prevention, biodiversity protection, and long-term soil health.
  • Develop national livestock genetic improvement programs combining conservation of indigenous breeds with advanced breeding technologies to improve productivity, disease resistance, and climate adaptation.
  • Prioritize development of precision livestock management systems, including sensor-based monitoring, automated feeding, health surveillance, AI-assisted herd management, and digital traceability.
  • Strengthen national veterinary surveillance, disease prevention, and biosecurity systems to protect domestic livestock populations against epidemic threats and safeguard food security.
  • Strengthen domestic production of veterinary medicines, vaccines, diagnostics, and animal-health technologies to reduce dependence on foreign supply chains.
  • Establish a National Feed Security Strategy to expand domestic production of animal feed crops, utilize agricultural by-products more efficiently, and support research into alternative feed technologies adapted to Mediterranean conditions.
  • Expand regional livestock-processing infrastructure, including slaughterhouses, dairy-processing facilities, refrigerated logistics, and meat-processing capacity, to strengthen domestic value creation and reduce transportation costs for producers.
  • Promote high-value domestic livestock products through advanced processing, certification, and international branding, particularly products associated with protected geographical indications and traditional Hellenic production.
  • Provide targeted financing, land-access mechanisms, housing support, and technical assistance for young livestock producers establishing or expanding productive enterprises.

Maritime Economy

  • Maintain Greek leadership in commercial shipping and maritime services.
  • Expand the merchant fleet, modernize ports, strengthen maritime logistics, and support productive economic activity on strategically important islands.
  • Develop an integrated national maritime economy linking shipping, ports, shipbuilding, ship repair, logistics, marine engineering, maritime finance, insurance, research, and advanced maritime technologies into coordinated industrial clusters.
  • Expand domestic shipbuilding, ship repair, maritime equipment manufacturing, autonomous maritime systems, and other high-value maritime industries.
  • Modernize ports, shipyards, fishing harbors, maritime logistics infrastructure, cold-chain facilities, and intermodal transport networks supporting both commercial activity and national resilience.
  • Develop offshore energy, offshore engineering, underwater robotics, marine surveying, and emerging maritime technology industries.
  • Promote sustainable fisheries and environmentally responsible aquaculture as strategic components of national food security, regional development, and export competitiveness.
  • Manage fisheries through continuous scientific assessment of fish stocks, marine ecosystems, and environmental conditions to ensure the long-term sustainability of marine resources.
  • Promote selective fishing technologies, digital catch monitoring, modern fishing equipment, and environmentally responsible harvesting practices that improve productivity while reducing ecological impact.
  • Expand technologically advanced aquaculture through modern production systems, streamlined regulation, scientific research, genetic improvement, disease management, and international market development.
  • Support marine biotechnology, pharmaceutical research, marine biomaterials, aquaculture innovation, and other high-value marine life-science industries.
  • Strengthen education, vocational training, and technology transfer across shipping, fisheries, aquaculture, shipbuilding, and maritime industries through cooperation between universities, research institutions, and productive enterprises.
  • Encourage comprehensive seabed mapping, hydrographic surveying, and marine scientific research to support navigation, environmental management, offshore infrastructure, resource assessment, and national maritime strategy.
  • Promote the responsible commercial utilization of marine resources consistent with long-term environmental sustainability and national strategic interests.
  • Maintain a competitive regulatory, legal, and tax environment supporting nationally registered shipping and maritime investment.

Strategic Tourism

  • Shift national tourism policy toward higher-value cultural, educational, medical, scientific, conference, and maritime tourism.
  • Reduce seasonal and regional concentration through diversification, sustainable visitor management and infrastructure planning.
  • Integrate tourism with cultural heritage, archaeological preservation, local production, environmental sustainability, and regional development.
  • Strengthen domestic value creation by integrating tourism with Hellenic agriculture, fisheries, manufacturing, traditional crafts, regional industries, and cultural production.
  • Expand cultural, archaeological, religious, scientific, educational, conference, and medical tourism through coordinated long-term national development strategies.

Innovation and Entrepreneurship

  • Reduce unnecessary administrative barriers to the creation and growth of new businesses.
  • Establish accelerated regulatory procedures for strategically significant productive investments while preserving legal certainty, environmental standards, and transparent oversight.
  • Support technology commercialization and university spin-offs in strategically important sectors.
  • Develop national innovation districts adjacent to major universities and research institutions to facilitate collaboration between academia, startups, established industry, and government.
  • Develop a National Talent Strategy to attract, retain, and repatriate highly skilled Hellenic professionals through research opportunities, entrepreneurship, competitive employment.
  • The National Forecasting Office should identify workforce capabilities, industrial labor shortages, and emerging technological requirements to guide education, industrial policy, and workforce planning.
  • Provide temporary regulatory assistance, legal support, patent assistance, and business incubation services for strategically important startups during their early stages of development.
  • Expand venture-capital financing for productive and innovative enterprises.
  • Prioritize innovative domestic firms in public procurement where consistent with competition and value-for-money principles.
  • Strengthen protection of intellectual property generated through publicly funded research.
  • Encourage accomplished members of the Greek diaspora to participate in national research, investment, entrepreneurship, and technology-transfer initiatives.
  • Develop competitive national capital markets capable of providing long-term financing for productive enterprises, technological innovation, and strategic industrial development alongside the banking system.
  • Establish legally guaranteed maximum approval periods for business formation, industrial licensing, and strategically important investments through fully digital administrative procedures.
  • Develop coordinated international branding strategies promoting Hellenic technology, manufacturing, scientific research, cultural industries, and high-value products in global markets.
  • Prioritize financing mechanisms for small and medium-sized productive enterprises that strengthen domestic supply chains, technological capability, regional development, and industrial resilience.

Economic Sovereignty

  • The Hellenic economy shall be organized to preserve the continuous functioning of the state and society under conditions of prolonged external disruption, international crisis, strategic embargo, financial isolation, total war, or major supply-chain interruption.
  • Preserve the institutional capacity to restore an independent national currency should this become necessary to safeguard national sovereignty, financial stability, or long-term strategic autonomy.
  • Maintain sufficient domestic productive capacity, financial institutions, payment systems, strategic reserves, and administrative capabilities to ensure the uninterrupted functioning of the national economy during severe international crises.
  • Ordinary public expenditure shall be financed primarily through recurring public revenue. Borrowing shall be reserved principally for national emergencies and investments creating durable productive assets or substantially strengthening the long-term capabilities of the nation.
  • Long-term public investment shall be financed primarily through sustained economic growth, sovereign investment returns, expenditure reprioritization, privatization of non-strategic public assets, and productivity improvements rather than persistent structural borrowing.
  • Require comprehensive long-term fiscal impact assessments for major legislation affecting public finances.
  • Regularly evaluate pension, healthcare, and welfare systems according to demographic trends, fiscal sustainability, and long-term national resilience.
  • Welcome productive foreign investment that strengthens domestic production, employment, technology transfer, scientific capability, and national development while preserving national control over strategically significant assets, infrastructure, and technologies.

Anti-Offshoring Measures

  • Penalize the relocation of production abroad.
  • Favor domestically produced goods and services in public procurement where consistent with quality, strategic resilience, and value for taxpayers.
  • Major strategic public procurement programs shall maximize domestic industrial participation, technology transfer, and long-term productive capability wherever economically and strategically feasible.
  • Prohibit strategic industries from relocating outside the country.
  • Require impact assessments for relocation of strategic production activities abroad, including employment and supply-chain effects.

Tax Policy

  • Taxation shall favor productive enterprise, technological innovation, industrial modernization, scientific research, family formation, domestic production, and long-term capital investment while discouraging speculation, rent extraction, excessive luxury consumption, environmentally harmful activities, and products imposing substantial public-health or social costs.
  • Provide targeted tax incentives for productive investment, industrial modernization, research and development, automation, advanced manufacturing, and businesses relocating production, research, or strategically significant operations to Greece.
  • Provide temporary regulatory and tax incentives encouraging the relocation of productive enterprises, industrial facilities, research centers, and strategic operations to Greece.
  • Maintain competitive taxation for productive enterprises during their establishment and early growth.
  • Adopt a strategically differentiated system of consumption taxation whereby tax rates are calibrated according to the long-term national interest, encouraging consumption that strengthens public health, demographic renewal, domestic production, environmental sustainability, and technological development while discouraging non-essential consumption, excessive luxury, and activities imposing disproportionate social or strategic costs.
  • Provide substantial tax relief for families with children, particularly large families, as part of the national demographic strategy.
  • Regularly adjust tax brackets and tax-free income thresholds to preserve purchasing power and prevent inflation-induced increases in effective taxation.
  • Strengthen taxation of gambling, games of chance, and activities generating substantial public-health or social costs.
  • Develop a fully integrated digital tax administration utilizing automated reporting, electronic invoicing, real-time transaction verification, AI-assisted auditing, and comprehensive financial traceability to combat tax evasion, smuggling, illicit financial flows, organized fraud, and large-scale economic crime.
  • Recovering revenue lost through tax evasion, organized fraud, corruption, and illicit financial activity shall constitute a primary source of fiscal strengthening before increases in the general tax burden are considered.
  • Simplify the tax system to improve transparency, compliance, predictability, and administrative efficiency.
  • Provide targeted fuel-tax relief or equivalent tax credits for verified work-related commuting where practical alternatives are unavailable, particularly in rural and peripheral regions.
  • Provide temporary tax incentives for employers that expand teleworking, hybrid work, and digital employment models where compatible with operational requirements, thereby reducing commuting costs, traffic congestion, energy consumption, and regional disparities.
  • Require periodic evaluation of all tax expenditures, exemptions, and incentives to ensure they continue serving their intended strategic purpose.

Labor Policy

  • Strengthen wage protections and labor standards through automated enforcement systems, real-time reporting of employment conditions, and enhanced inspection capacity.
  • Ensure transparent, digital recording of working hours, overtime, and contractual conditions, with interoperable reporting across employers, tax authorities, and social insurance systems.
  • Promote teleworking and hybrid work models where compatible with the nature of the activity, particularly in administrative, research, education, and knowledge-intensive sectors.
  • Strengthen mechanisms against undeclared and under-declared work through coordinated labor, tax, and social security enforcement using data-driven audit systems.
  • Extend maternity and paternity leave and develop comprehensive family-support policies that facilitate workforce participation and demographic renewal.
  • Expand disability assistance and social integration programs.
  • Require structured employee representation mechanisms in medium and large enterprises, including worker councils or equivalent consultative bodies, adapted to sectoral characteristics and firm size.
  • Promote skills development and continuous training programs linked to labor market needs, particularly in strategic sectors, through cooperation between the state, employers, and educational institutions.

Market Competition and Economic Concentration

  • Prevent excessive concentration in strategically important sectors, including food distribution, energy, telecommunications, banking, and transportation.
  • Strengthen competition authorities and anti-cartel enforcement mechanisms.
  • Require periodic competition reviews of strategically important sectors where market concentration exceeds thresholds established by law.
  • Increase transparency in pricing practices throughout critical supply chains.
  • Establish real-time market surveillance systems for essential goods sectors to detect cartel behavior, abnormal pricing patterns, and supply-chain manipulation.
  • Create structured whistleblower channels with incentive mechanisms for reporting anti-competitive practices, coordinated pricing, or supply restriction behavior in essential markets.
  • Establish coordinated monitoring of essential goods distribution chains (food, fuel, pharmaceuticals) to detect and prevent organized manipulation, hoarding, or artificial scarcity creation during crises.
  • Support producer cooperatives and regional distribution networks capable of competing with dominant market actors.
  • Monitor excessive profit margins in sectors essential to household welfare.
  • Preserve market competition while preventing private monopolies and oligopolies from acquiring undue economic or political influence.

Market Transparency and Consumer Protection

  • Require clear origin and content labeling standards for food and consumer goods.
  • Strengthen enforcement against food adulteration, origin fraud, counterfeit geographical indications, and product mislabeling through advanced traceability systems, laboratory testing, and coordinated domestic and international enforcement.
  • Increase penalties for fraud, deceptive practices, and corporate misconduct.
  • Strengthen consumer rights in digital and financial markets.

Housing Policy

  • Prioritize owner-occupation and family formation over financial speculation.
  • Expand construction of family-sized housing.
  • Provide preferential financing for first-home purchases by families.
  • Strengthen protections for primary residence ownership by expanding structured, state-supervised debt restructuring mechanisms for households facing systemic financial distress
  • Introduce targeted safeguards against speculative concentration of residential housing in high-demand zones, including monitoring of acquisition patterns and regulatory intervention mechanisms where housing stock shifts disproportionately toward non-occupancy investment use among eligible owners.
  • Establish public-private housing partnerships in strategically important regions.
V. Energy and Resource Security

Civilizations do not collapse only through invasion. They also collapse through dependence. Energy insecurity is strategic vulnerability in its purest form.

Energy Independence

  • Reassess and partially repeal environmental regulations deemed incompatible with energy security.
  • Maintain and modernize domestic lignite generation as a strategic reserve capability until alternative domestic sources can fully guarantee national energy security.
  • Invest in domestic synthetic-fuel production and advanced oil-refining capabilities.
  • Reduce strategic dependence on external energy suppliers and volatile international energy markets.
  • Maintain public ownership or effective national control over strategically essential energy infrastructure, particularly electricity-transmission and distribution networks.
  • Establish a Social Energy Stability Mechanism ensuring predictable household-level energy pricing during periods of external market volatility, operating within the framework of nationally controlled energy production and distribution systems.
  • Reform electricity-market pricing mechanisms to better reflect the actual cost of domestic energy production while reducing excessive exposure to international price volatility.
  • Increase transparency in electricity pricing by requiring public disclosure of the principal components contributing to retail energy costs.
  • Strengthen competition and regulatory oversight within wholesale and retail electricity markets to prevent market distortions, abuse of dominant positions, and excessive pricing.
  • Expand domestic electricity-generation capacity sufficient to maintain national resilience during major crises.
  • Encourage households, businesses, municipalities, and local communities to develop distributed energy generation.
  • Promote the long-term modernization and energy efficiency of residential, commercial, industrial, and public buildings, prioritizing schools, hospitals, critical public infrastructure, and energy-intensive industries, to reduce national energy consumption and strengthen energy security.
  • Strengthen national energy grids against cyber-attacks, sabotage, natural disasters, and wartime disruption.
  • Modernize transmission and distribution networks to accommodate distributed generation, large-scale storage, electrification of transport and industry, and future increases in domestic electricity production.
  • Ensure internationally competitive and predictable energy costs for strategically important industrial sectors through long-term national energy planning.
  • Maintain sufficient dispatchable domestic generation capacity capable of guaranteeing uninterrupted electricity supply during periods of low renewable output or national emergency.

Strategic Energy Diversification

  • Develop a diversified energy portfolio combining fossil fuels, renewable energy, energy storage, and emerging technologies.
  • Establish a National Energy Innovation Program coordinating universities, research institutes, industry, and government to accelerate the development and commercialization of strategic energy technologies.
  • Support research, development, and commercialization of advanced energy technologies, including next-generation storage, hydrogen, geothermal energy, synthetic fuels, carbon capture, and marine energy.
  • Accelerate exploration and development of Greece's geothermal resources for electricity generation, district heating, industrial applications, and island energy systems.
  • Maintain adequate domestic natural-gas infrastructure, storage capacity, and flexible generation capabilities to support grid stability, industrial competitiveness, and national resilience during the energy transition.
  • Support the development of wave, tidal, and other marine renewable-energy technologies where economically and geographically viable.
  • Expand renewable-energy generation where it strengthens national self-sufficiency and grid resilience.
  • Prioritize energy-storage technologies capable of supporting intermittent power generation.
  • Accelerate the deployment of utility-scale energy-storage infrastructure, including pumped hydroelectric storage, advanced battery systems, thermal storage, hydrogen storage, and other emerging technologies.
  • Ensure that new renewable-energy capacity is accompanied by sufficient storage and grid infrastructure to maximize domestic utilization of electricity generation.
  • Encourage distributed household and community battery-storage systems to improve local resilience and reduce pressure on the national grid.
  • Encourage domestic production of renewable-energy components and related technologies.
  • Encourage domestic manufacturing of transformers, high-voltage equipment, power electronics, batteries, electrical machinery, and other critical energy-system components.
  • Maintain a continuously updated national assessment of renewable-energy resources to guide long-term infrastructure planning and investment.
  • Evaluate all energy investments according to long-term strategic value rather than ideological preference.
  • Develop integrated energy strategies for island regions combining local renewable generation, energy storage, smart grids, interconnections where economically justified, and resilient water infrastructure to reduce dependence on imported fuels.
  • Monitor developments in advanced civilian nuclear technologies, including small modular reactors and next-generation reactors, and preserve Greece's ability to adopt such technologies if they become strategically beneficial.
  • Expand nuclear engineering, reactor science, and radiation technologies within Greek universities and research institutions to strengthen national scientific capabilities.
  • Prioritize domestic storage and value-added utilization of surplus electricity before exporting excess generation to foreign markets.
  • Expand national charging infrastructure and alternative-fuel networks where they strengthen domestic energy security and reduce dependence on imported petroleum.
  • Expand strategic electricity and natural-gas interconnections with neighboring countries where they strengthen Greece's energy security, regional influence, and export potential.

Offshore Resources and Maritime Energy

  • Defend Greece's sovereign rights to explore and develop offshore energy resources within its territorial waters and maritime zones.
  • Accelerate offshore geological surveying, seabed mapping, and exploration of maritime hydrocarbon and mineral deposits.
  • Develop domestic capabilities for offshore extraction, processing, and transportation of strategic resources.
  • Reduce dependence on foreign suppliers for critical maritime-energy infrastructure.
  • Position Greece as a strategic energy hub for Southeastern Europe through the development of export infrastructure, regional interconnections, and secure maritime energy corridors.

Water Security

  • Construct long-term reservoir infrastructure.
  • Implement continuous aquifer monitoring.
  • Expand desalination projects, particularly for island communities.
  • Integrate renewable-powered desalination and water recycling into long-term water-security planning for island and water-stressed regions.

Climate Resilience

  • Prepare national infrastructure, settlements, and resource systems for long-term climatic change.
  • Expand drought-mitigation measures, water-storage capacity, and irrigation efficiency.
  • Strengthen coastal protection and adaptation planning for vulnerable regions.
  • Integrate climate-risk assessments into infrastructure, defense, agricultural, and demographic planning.

Strategic Minerals and Resource Security

  • Identify and secure domestic sources of critical minerals required for defense, energy, industrial, and technological production.
  • Expand geological surveying and resource mapping of mainland and island territories.
  • Prevent foreign control of strategically significant resource deposits.

National Strategic Reserves

  • Maintain strategic reserves of fuel, medicines, food, industrial inputs, rare earth materials, and critical technologies.
  • Regularly audit and rotate reserves to ensure readiness.
  • Gradually increase national holdings of gold and other strategic reserve assets.
  • Diversify reserve holdings to reduce vulnerability to external financial shocks.

Environmental Stewardship

  • Protect forests, agricultural land, water resources, and ecosystems as strategic national assets.
  • Expand reforestation initiatives.
  • Use drone surveillance to strengthen wildfire prevention during high-risk periods.
  • Enforce anti-pollution measures when environmental degradation threatens agriculture, tourism, or critical infrastructure.
  • Establish monitoring programs for invasive terrestrial and marine species. Support research and commercial utilization of invasive species where economically viable.
  • Develop rapid-response mechanisms to protect agriculture, fisheries, ecosystems, and public health.
  • Encourage the productive use of agricultural, livestock, and organic waste for energy generation, reducing pollution while strengthening rural economies.
  • Promote recovery and reuse of strategic raw materials through advanced recycling and circular-economy technologies.

Scientific and technological leadership will determine the global balance of power in the twenty-first century.

Research Policy

VII. Education

Education determines the quality of future citizens, administrators, scientists, and leaders. A civilization cannot outperform the institutions that train its people.

Educational Mission

  • The purpose of education is to cultivate knowledgeable, disciplined, and responsible citizens while identifying and developing each individual's abilities to their fullest potential.
  • A meritocratic education system shall ensure that advancement into positions of professional and public responsibility is determined by demonstrated competence, character, and achievement rather than privilege or political influence.
  • Promote lifelong learning, adult education, and professional reskilling throughout working life.
  • Cultivate integrity, perseverance, self-discipline, responsibility, respect for law, intellectual honesty, long-term thinking, and commitment to public service as essential civic virtues.
  • Organize education to identify, cultivate, and deploy the full range of human talent in service of individual fulfillment and national development.

Educational Structure

  • The primary objective of general education shall be to provide every student with a strong common foundation while progressively identifying and developing individual talents through a unified national system of merit-based assessment.
  • Establish a nationwide system of annual standardized examinations beginning in primary education to measure knowledge, aptitude, reasoning, and achievement throughout compulsory education.
  • Beginning in the second year of primary education, students shall be grouped into educational cohorts according to demonstrated academic performance, allowing instruction to be adapted to differing levels of ability while maintaining common national standards.
  • Students shall remain eligible throughout their education to move between cohorts through subsequent national examinations, ensuring that educational advancement reflects demonstrated performance rather than early placement.
  • National examinations shall include specialized assessments designed to identify scientific, technical, artistic, athletic, entrepreneurial, leadership, and other exceptional abilities that may not be fully reflected in general academic performance.
  • Educational specialization shall progressively increase as students mature, with pathways emphasizing scientific, technical, classical, vocational, artistic, entrepreneurial, athletic, military, leadership, or other areas of demonstrated aptitude.

Meritocratic Advancement

  • Educational advancement, cohort placement, and access to specialized educational opportunities shall reflect demonstrated competence and achievement rather than age alone.
  • Admission to higher education shall be determined through a weighted cumulative record of national examination performance accumulated throughout compulsory and secondary education rather than a single final examination.
  • Provide accelerated educational pathways and specialized institutions for exceptionally high-performing students.
  • Develop specialized preparatory pathways for students seeking careers requiring the highest levels of public responsibility, including the competitive national examinations for senior public administration.
  • Provide continuous educational and career guidance enabling students to pursue academic, vocational, technical, artistic, entrepreneurial, military, or public-service careers according to demonstrated aptitude and personal aspiration.
  • Strengthen vocational education as an equally respected pathway for students whose demonstrated abilities or professional ambitions are better served through technical and applied education.

Curriculum and Student Development

  • Cultivate civic responsibility, constitutional literacy, national history, cultural heritage, public service, and social responsibility throughout compulsory education.
  • Cultivate alignment with the National Historical Mission.
  • Strengthen instruction in mathematics, the natural sciences, engineering, computer science, Ancient Greek, modern languages, history and philosophy as essential components of a rigorous national curriculum.
  • Introduce practical life-skills education including financial literacy, budgeting, taxation, consumer rights, contracts, first aid, and digital citizenship.
  • Promote lifelong physical fitness through high-quality physical education, sports participation, nutrition education, and healthy lifestyles.
  • Include military, patriotic, traditional, and classical musical traditions within music education as part of Greece's broader cultural and historical heritage.
  • Maintain orderly, disciplined, and respectful learning environments that enable effective teaching and academic achievement.
  • Develop advanced analytical and strategic reasoning within specialized academic tracks.
  • Teach digital literacy, media literacy, cybersecurity awareness, responsible use of artificial intelligence, and protection against online manipulation as essential components of modern citizenship.
  • Expand high-quality early childhood education emphasizing language development, socialization, foundational numeracy and literacy, and early identification of developmental needs.
  • Strengthen special education, accessibility, individualized support, and early intervention for students with disabilities and learning differences.
  • Provide continuous educational and career guidance enabling students to make informed decisions regarding academic, vocational, and professional pathways.
  • Encourage active cooperation between schools and families in supporting students' academic development, character formation, and educational aspirations.

Educational Quality

  • Align curricula, examinations, and classroom instruction so that successful academic progression does not depend upon private tutoring.
  • Regularly review curricula to ensure relevance, rigor, and national priorities.
  • Evaluate educational institutions according to measurable student outcomes.
  • Ensure safe, well-maintained and resilient educational facilities.
  • Modernize school infrastructure.
  • Prevent violence, intimidation and vandalism.
  • Ensure that students throughout Greece, including remote, rural, and disadvantaged communities, have equal access to high-quality public education and merit-based educational advancement.

Vocational and Technical Education

  • Expand technical schools aligned with national industrial priorities.
  • Strengthen vocational pathways in manufacturing, agriculture, shipbuilding, energy, healthcare, and information technology.
  • Promote apprenticeships and partnerships between educational institutions and industry.
  • Improve the quality, prestige, and economic value of vocational education.

Higher Education

  • Strengthen public universities as centers of scientific excellence, innovation, and national development.
  • Allocate public funding according to transparent measures including educational quality, research performance, graduate outcomes, strategic national priorities, and international competitiveness.
  • Strengthen cooperation between universities, research institutes, industry, healthcare institutions, and government to accelerate knowledge transfer and innovation.
  • Protect universities as places of learning, research, and lawful debate while ensuring safe campuses free from criminal activity and political intimidation.
  • Expand undergraduate research opportunities, doctoral education, international scientific collaboration, and technology commercialization.
  • Align educational capacity and publicly funded degree programs with long-term national workforce, demographic, industrial, and research priorities while preserving institutional academic freedom in teaching and research.
  • Align educational capacity and publicly funded degree programs with long-term national workforce, demographic, industrial, and research priorities while preserving institutional academic freedom in teaching and research.
  • Expand merit-based scholarships, research fellowships, and financial support for high-performing students regardless of socioeconomic background.

Public Leadership and State Administration

  • Develop specialized educational pathways preparing students for careers in public administration, diplomacy, national security, strategic planning, and other positions of national responsibility.
  • Ensure that preparation for competitive national public-service examinations begins sufficiently early to allow talented students to develop the knowledge, analytical ability, and administrative competence required for senior state service.
  • Maintain open access to national public-service examinations for all citizens regardless of age or educational background while providing structured educational pathways for those wishing to prepare through the formal education system.

Teaching Profession

  • Recruit teachers through transparent merit-based procedures.
  • Provide incentives for educators serving in remote regions.
  • Expand continuous professional development, pedagogical training, digital competencies, and mentoring throughout teaching careers.
  • Implement permanent performance evaluations. Evaluate educators using multiple measures including classroom practice, student progress, professional development, institutional contribution, and student achievement.
  • Reward outstanding teaching performance and educational outcomes.
VIII. Healthcare and Social Welfare

The state exists not only to defend borders, but to preserve the physical and social health of its people.

Healthcare System

  • Guarantee universal access to preventive healthcare services.
  • Strengthen primary healthcare through fully staffed health centers providing preventive medicine, chronic-disease management, emergency stabilization, and referral services.
  • Provide universal access to regular preventive health examinations and expand nationwide evidence-based screening programs targeting cardiovascular disease, cancer, diabetes, obesity, addiction, mental illness, and other major causes of preventable mortality.
  • Fully digitalize medical records and integrate healthcare information systems across all public medical institutions.
  • Develop integrated national digital referral and appointment systems to improve patient access, optimize waiting lists, and strengthen coordination across healthcare providers.
  • Expand healthcare access in rural regions, islands, and other underserved communities.
  • Strengthen family medicine as the foundation of primary healthcare, ensuring that every citizen has timely access to continuous, coordinated medical care.
  • Maintain sufficient intensive-care, emergency-care, and specialized treatment capacity to ensure national readiness during both routine operation and large-scale health emergencies.
  • Develop an integrated national medical transport network combining ground, air, and maritime emergency services to ensure rapid access to specialized care throughout the country.
  • Evaluate hospitals according to standardized, risk-adjusted performance indicators including treatment outcomes, waiting times, patient safety, financial efficiency, and patient satisfaction.
  • Evaluate hospital administrators according to measurable institutional performance rather than seniority or political appointment.
  • Establish maximum national waiting-time standards for diagnostic examinations, specialist consultations, and elective procedures, with regular public reporting of performance.
  • Increase national mental-health capacity through expanded psychiatric services, early intervention, and community-based care.
  • Expand infrastructure and specialized services for elderly care.
  • Expand home-based healthcare, rehabilitation, palliative care, and long-term nursing services to improve quality of life for older persons, patients with chronic illnesses, and individuals recovering from major illness or injury.
  • Strengthen pediatric healthcare, school-health services, developmental screening, adolescent medicine, childhood nutrition, mental-health support, and the early identification of developmental disorders.
  • Maintain comprehensive evidence-based immunization programs throughout all stages of life, including childhood, adulthood, and older age, while ensuring resilient national vaccine supply chains and informed public participation.
  • Expand telemedicine and remote specialist consultation services to improve healthcare access in islands, mountainous regions, and other underserved communities.
  • Strengthen women's healthcare throughout all stages of life, including maternal health, preventive screening, reproductive medicine, menopause care, and the early diagnosis and treatment of conditions disproportionately affecting women.

Healthcare Workforce

  • Provide housing assistance, financial incentives, and accelerated career advancement for healthcare professionals serving in remote islands, border regions, and underserved communities.
  • Improve incentives for nurses through higher pay, improved working conditions, career-development opportunities, and earlier retirement options for occupations involving exceptionally demanding clinical responsibilities.
  • Expand opportunities for continuing professional education, specialist training, and international scientific exchange throughout medical careers.
  • Recruit and retain highly qualified healthcare professionals through competitive working conditions and merit-based advancement.
  • Regularly assess national workforce requirements across medical specialties and align specialist training capacity with long-term healthcare needs.

Strategic Health Security

  • Expand domestic pharmaceutical, biotechnology, vaccine, active pharmaceutical ingredient, and medical-equipment production, prioritizing strategically essential medicines and critical medical supplies where economically sustainable.
  • Maintain strategic national reserves of medicines, vaccines, medical equipment, blood products, and emergency medical supplies.
  • Strengthen preparedness for pandemics, biological threats, mass-casualty incidents, and other national health emergencies.
  • Ensure the resilience of healthcare infrastructure against cyber-attacks, natural disasters, supply disruptions, and wartime conditions.
  • Establish measurable national health objectives including life expectancy, healthy life expectancy, fertility, infant mortality, obesity, preventable diseases, mental-health outcomes, and healthcare-system performance.
  • Maintain scalable national surge capacity allowing hospitals to rapidly expand intensive-care, emergency, and infectious-disease treatment during national crises.
  • Establish legal frameworks enabling the temporary mobilization and coordinated integration of private healthcare resources during national emergencies, subject to fair compensation and clearly defined emergency powers.

Public Health and National Vitality

  • Promote physical fitness, healthy nutrition, preventive medicine, and active lifestyles through long-term national initiatives.
  • Expand access to public sports facilities, walking routes, cycling infrastructure, and recreational spaces.
  • Reduce the long-term burden of chronic disease through early diagnosis, health education, and evidence-based prevention.
  • Promote voluntary blood, plasma, stem-cell, and organ donation through sustained national awareness campaigns and public education.
  • Encourage active and productive aging through preventive healthcare, lifelong physical activity, and community participation.
  • Strengthen prevention, treatment, rehabilitation, and reintegration programs for substance abuse and other forms of addiction.
  • Integrate public-health objectives into education, urban planning, workplace policy, environmental policy, and demographic strategy.
  • Integrate health education, nutrition, preventive medicine, and first-aid training throughout the national education system.
  • Deploy digital technologies and artificial intelligence to improve disease surveillance, preventive medicine, resource allocation, and healthcare-system productivity while maintaining appropriate safeguards.
  • Expand specialized services supporting patients with dementia and other neurodegenerative diseases, while providing practical assistance and respite services for family caregivers.
  • Promote workplace health programs encouraging preventive healthcare, physical activity, mental well-being, and healthy lifestyles across both public and private sectors.
  • Combat antimicrobial resistance through responsible prescribing, infection prevention, surveillance, public education, and support for the development of new antimicrobial therapies.

Medical Research and Innovation

  • Support research into longevity, fertility, age-related diseases, genetic medicine, biotechnology, public health, and emerging medical technologies.
  • Promote precision medicine, genomics, and personalized healthcare where scientifically validated and consistent with ethical standards.
  • Strengthen Greece's international leadership in medically assisted reproduction, reproductive medicine, and fertility research while maintaining high ethical and scientific standards.
  • Expand clinical research and international clinical trials by simplifying regulatory procedures while maintaining rigorous oversight.
  • Strengthen cooperation between hospitals, universities, research institutes, and innovative enterprises to accelerate the translation of scientific discoveries into clinical practice.
  • Prioritize the domestic commercialization of publicly funded biomedical research where consistent with national strategic interests.
  • Promote Greece as a regional center for medical research, biotechnology, advanced clinical treatment, and high-value medical tourism.
  • Support scientific research into Greece's medicinal plants, natural products, and bioactive compounds for pharmaceutical, biotechnology, and medical applications.

Healthcare Governance

  • Ensure transparent procurement of medicines, medical equipment, and healthcare services.
  • Strengthen anti-corruption enforcement throughout the healthcare system, eliminate informal payments through transparent digital administrative procedures, and impose severe penalties for bribery, procurement fraud, and abuse of public medical resources.
  • Expand electronic prescribing, promote evidence-based prescribing practices, increase the use of high-quality generic medicines where clinically appropriate, and strengthen price negotiations for essential medicines.
  • Reduce unnecessary administrative burdens through digitalization and process simplification.
  • Base healthcare policy on measurable outcomes, scientific evidence, and long-term public-health objectives.
  • Protect medical privacy through secure health-information systems, strong cybersecurity, transparent governance, patient rights, and responsible use of anonymized health data for scientific research.
IX. Infrastructure and Transportation

Roads, transport systems, and urban planning shape daily life more than most legislation. Functional infrastructure is civilization made visible.

Infrastructure Governance

  • Prioritize public infrastructure projects through transparent national cost-benefit analysis, resilience, strategic importance, and long-term economic value rather than political considerations.
  • Prioritize the maintenance, modernization, and efficient operation of existing infrastructure before undertaking unnecessary expansion.
  • Adopt uniform national engineering, resilience, interoperability, and maintenance standards for critical public infrastructure.
  • Complete a unified National Digital Property Platform integrating cadastral, planning, environmental, infrastructure, and permitting information into a single national system.
  • Require public disclosure of all major public-works contracts.
  • Require major public infrastructure projects to complete technical studies, environmental approvals, land acquisition, and financing before construction contracts are awarded.
  • Establish strict completion deadlines and performance penalties for contractors.
  • Impose statutory deadlines for public authorities to complete permitting, environmental reviews, expropriations, and administrative approvals for nationally significant infrastructure projects.
  • Publish real-time progress tracking for major infrastructure projects.
  • Introduce mandatory lifecycle asset-management systems, digital maintenance records, and long-term maintenance planning for all critical public infrastructure.
  • Permit contract renegotiations only under clearly defined exceptional circumstances, subject to independent review, full public disclosure, and oversight by the competent constitutional authorities where appropriate.
  • Strengthen competition in public procurement by preventing excessive market concentration, anti-competitive practices, and cartelization in strategic infrastructure projects.
  • Introduce independent post-completion audits for major infrastructure projects.
  • Require long-term maintenance funding to be incorporated into the financial planning of major public infrastructure projects.
  • Mandate periodic resilience assessments of nationally significant infrastructure against military, cyber, climatic, seismic, and demographic risks.
  • Develop digital models of critical infrastructure to improve maintenance planning, emergency response, and long-term investment decisions.
  • Coordinate utility installation and maintenance to minimize repeated excavation, reduce costs, and improve long-term urban infrastructure quality.
  • Encourage the use of advanced construction technologies, digital engineering, modular construction, and domestic innovation to improve infrastructure quality and reduce project costs.

Strategic Infrastructure

  • Develop long-term national infrastructure plans extending beyond electoral cycles.
  • Expand strategic energy, water, transportation, and communications infrastructure.
  • Strengthen infrastructure resilience against war, cyber-attacks, natural disasters, and supply-chain disruptions.
  • Construct redundant infrastructure systems for critical national functions.
  • Strengthen secure national digital infrastructure, including strategically important domestic data-center capacity.
  • Prevent foreign ownership or operational control of nationally critical infrastructure where such control would endanger national security or strategic autonomy.
  • Develop nationally distributed strategic logistics hubs and supply-chain infrastructure capable of maintaining essential economic and military logistics during national emergencies.
  • Protect strategic transportation, energy, communications, and water corridors from incompatible development through long-term national spatial planning.
  • Expand flood-protection infrastructure, modernize drainage systems, and improve stormwater management in vulnerable urban and rural areas.
  • Modernize integrated freight and logistics corridors connecting strategic ports, airports, industrial zones, railways, logistics hubs, and border regions to strengthen Greece's role as a regional transportation gateway.
  • Expand emergency communications, evacuation routes, wildfire-support infrastructure, emergency operations facilities, and disaster-response infrastructure.
  • Modernize national water distribution, wastewater, electricity, gas, and telecommunications networks to improve efficiency, resilience, and long-term reliability.
  • Prioritize underground installation of critical electricity and telecommunications infrastructure where economically and strategically justified.

Mobility and Urban Planning

  • Promote urban planning that balances density, accessibility, quality of life, and long-term sustainability.
  • Expand strategically located parking infrastructure, including underground facilities and park-and-ride facilities connected to major public transportation networks.
  • Develop a nationwide real-time traffic monitoring and guidance system.
  • Increase investment in public transportation capacity, reliability, accessibility, fleet modernization, and service frequency.
  • Complete nationwide deployment of modern signalling, automatic train protection, centralized traffic management, digital safety systems, and high-capacity freight connections across the national railway network.
  • Address national transport bottlenecks through targeted road construction.

National Connectivity

  • Expand high-speed digital connectivity throughout the country.
  • Improve transportation links between major cities, border regions, and islands.
  • Strengthen multimodal transport links between islands, mainland regions, and strategic border areas to improve economic integration and national resilience.
  • Eliminate infrastructure disparities between urban, rural, and island regions.
  • Maintain and modernize regional airports where they strengthen national connectivity, tourism, emergency response, and access to island and border regions.
  • Ensure year-round minimum transportation connectivity for remote islands, mountainous communities, and strategically important border regions.
X. Immigration and Citizenship

Borders define the boundaries of political community. Immigration policy must serve the long-term interests of the nation, not short-term ideological preferences.

Border Security

  • Prioritize the prevention of unlawful entry over post-entry administrative processing.
  • Maintain effective control of national land and maritime borders through surveillance systems, physical infrastructure, maritime patrols, and rapid-response capabilities.
  • Expand personnel, equipment, surveillance capabilities, and operational readiness of the Hellenic Coast Guard.
  • Expand national capacity for the identification, processing, detention, and removal of unlawful entrants.
  • Accelerate deportation procedures for individuals lacking a legal right to remain within the country.
  • Negotiate readmission agreements with countries of origin and transit.
  • Prioritize dismantling international human-smuggling organizations through intelligence cooperation, financial investigations, and international law-enforcement partnerships.
  • Reduce incentives for unlawful immigration through strict enforcement of employment, residency, and documentation requirements.
  • Increase inspections and penalties against employers who knowingly hire unauthorized workers or facilitate illegal labor markets.

Refugee Policy

  • Prioritize eventual voluntary or assisted repatriation whenever conditions permit.
  • Concentrate assistance on basic necessities, healthcare, and security rather than long-term welfare dependency.
  • Ensure refugee assistance does not exceed the economic position of low-income Greek citizens.
  • Locate refugee accommodation according to national security, demographic, and administrative considerations.
  • Distinguish clearly between temporary refuge and permanent settlement.

Legal Immigration

  • Permit legal immigration only where it serves clearly identified national economic, demographic, scientific, or strategic interests.
  • Require employers to demonstrate inability to recruit sufficient domestic labor before obtaining foreign-worker permits.
  • Ensure foreign labor does not undermine domestic wages, working conditions, or employment opportunities.
  • Require foreign workers to maintain private health insurance and contribute to national social-insurance systems.
  • Require legal immigrants to demonstrate respect for Greek laws and institutions.
  • Require demonstrated economic self-sufficiency for long-term residence.
  • Establish a Civic Residency Contribution for non-citizen permanent residents in recognition of the benefits and protections provided by the Hellenic state.
  • Reserve political authority and constitutional privileges primarily for citizens.

Citizenship and Civic Status

  • Replace the binary citizenship model with multiple tiers of civic status reflecting differing degrees of political participation, rights, obligations, and integration.
  • Full civic status shall remain linked to pure intergenerational membership within the Hellenic community.
  • Ownership of land and strategically significant real estate shall be reserved to civic-status categories designated by law.
  • All civic-status categories may lease residential or commercial property subject to conditions established by law.
  • Marriage shall not create any automatic entitlement to civic-status advancement.
  • Exceptions to ordinary civic-status rules shall be permitted only under exceptional circumstances and according to criteria established by constitutional law.
  • Pursue renegotiation of relevant European agreements where necessary.

Assimilation and Cultural Integration

  • Prioritize the preservation of the historical, cultural, and demographic continuity of the Hellenic nation.
  • Encourage cultural assimilation into the Hellenic national community while maintaining public order and social cohesion.
  • Reject the formation of parallel societies and permanently segregated communities.
XI. Culture, Identity, and Religion

Material prosperity alone cannot sustain a civilization. Nations endure through culture, memory, identity, and purpose.

Civilizational Identity

  • Promote awareness of Hellenic history as a continuous civilizational tradition linking Classical Greece, Byzantium, the modern Greek state, and the Fourth Hellenic Civilization.
  • Strengthen historical consciousness through education, museums, public commemorations, media, and cultural institutions.
  • Encourage citizens to view family formation, public service, military service, scientific achievement, and civic responsibility as contributions to a shared civilizational mission.
  • Preserve and develop national symbols, ceremonies, and traditions that reinforce social cohesion and historical continuity.
  • Preserve, enrich, and promote the Greek language as a foundational pillar of Hellenic civilization.

Cultural Policy

  • Support domestic film, television, documentary, digital media, animation, and interactive entertainment industries that strengthen national identity and explore lesser-known chapters of Hellenic history.
  • Promote local traditions, customs, and regional cultural heritage through national media and educational initiatives.
  • Support the preservation and active transmission of intangible cultural heritage - including traditional music, dance, crafts, festivals, oral traditions, and regional customs - through educational programs, apprenticeships, and community initiatives.
  • Encourage architectural standards that preserve the historical character, aesthetic quality, and regional architectural traditions of towns and villages while permitting appropriate modern development.
  • Encourage volunteer organizations, local associations, charitable initiatives, and community institutions that strengthen civic responsibility and social cohesion.
  • Organize international competitions in Ancient Greek language, philosophy, history, and classical studies.
  • Expand student choirs, orchestras, theatre groups, debate societies, and cultural organizations throughout the educational system.
  • Provide support for artists, architects, musicians, writers, and scholars producing original work rooted in Hellenic traditions.
  • Encourage alternatives to consumerism, cultural homogenization, and excessive dependence on globalized mass culture through public cultural initiatives.
  • Promote domestic consumption of essential cultural and informational goods through public awareness programs supporting local production ecosystems in food, media, and basic consumer goods sectors, reinforcing resilience-oriented consumption patterns.
  • Support the preservation and development of traditional crafts, architecture, and clothing.

International Cultural Influence

  • Promote Hellenic culture, philosophy, language, and historical achievements through cultural diplomacy and international educational programs.
  • Support creators, researchers, educators, and public figures who strengthen Greece's global cultural presence.
  • Establish international centers dedicated to the study of Hellenic civilization.
  • Strengthen cooperation with the Greek diaspora in promoting Hellenic culture, scientific achievement, entrepreneurship, and international cultural diplomacy.
  • Expand Greek-language education abroad and strengthen ties with philhellenic communities worldwide.
  • Develop digital platforms and virtual museums that make Hellenic history, philosophy, literature, and cultural heritage accessible worldwide.

Media and Information

  • Strengthen resilience against foreign information warfare, influence operations, and coordinated disinformation campaigns.
  • Increase transparency regarding foreign funding and ownership of media organizations.
  • Encourage the development of independent Greek digital platforms, media organizations, and content-production capabilities.

Religious Policy

  • Recognize the Orthodox Church as a foundational institution of Hellenic historical continuity, cultural identity, and social cohesion.
  • Encourage religious institutions to strengthen family stability, civic responsibility, charitable service, historical continuity, and national cohesion in partnership with the Hellenic state.
  • Establish a network of ecclesiastical representatives attached to Greek diplomatic missions in Orthodox-majority countries.
  • Establish church-supported restoration and preservation programs for monasteries, churches, manuscripts, temples, and sacred sites.
  • Encourage religious institutions to participate in elderly care, charitable assistance, disaster relief, and community support.
  • Require financial transparency for major religious institutions.
  • Encourage modest lifestyles and charitable stewardship among senior religious leadership.
  • Convene Ecumenical Councils and other pan-Orthodox assemblies in Greece in order to strengthen the international role of Hellenic Orthodoxy, promote dialogue within the Orthodox world, and reinforce Greece's cultural and diplomatic influence.
Restoration is not the reconstruction of the past. It is the disciplined creation of a stronger future.
