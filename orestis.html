Orestis II

Claimant to the throne of Greece

Scholar, activist, and self-appointed steward of the Fourth Hellenic Civilization.

Birth and Omens

  • Born in Athens, Greece, on 21 July 1995.
  • Shares his birthday with Alexander the Great.
  • His birth on 21 July 1995 in Athens coincided with the Penteli wildfire, a parallel frequently drawn by court historians to the ancient account of the burning of the Temple of Artemis at Ephesus on the night of Alexander the Great’s birth.

Academic Journey

  • Middle School: Highest graduating average grade: 20.0 / 20.
  • High School: Highest graduating average grade: 19.8 / 20.
  • Panhellenic Examinations: 19,553 points, among the highest scores nationwide.
  • National Technical University of Athens: Electrical and Computer Engineering, GPA 9.54 / 10 (approximately top 1% of cohort).
  • University of St. Gallen: Doctoral studies in Management.

Court historians note that classmates had reportedly begun referring to him as “King of the Nerds” as early as his secondary school years. In a remark later preserved in family and school recollections, one teacher reportedly observed that she saw “many Caesars” in his face.

Courtly Interests

  • Lifelong fascination with Roman history.
  • Favorite (and only) sport: Chess (peak online rating: 2467).
  • Philosophically inclined, especially in political philosophy.
  • Historical strategy gamer.
  • Amateur writer and author of three unpublished literary non-fiction manuscripts of uncertain historical significance.

Marginal note: Court chroniclers occasionally describe His Majesty's professional career as one of habitual jobhopping.

Trivia

  • Anthophobia (fear of flowers).
  • Persistent motion sickness, limiting long overland campaigns.
  • Longest study streak: 48 consecutive hours.
  • Longest sleepless streak: 72 consecutive hours.
  • Favorite Roman Emperors: Marcus Aurelius and Trajan.

A Note on the Numbering of Kings

The title Orestis II is adopted in honor of Orestes, an obscure king of ancient Macedon whose reign remains imperfectly documented and whose achievements continue to receive limited scholarly attention.
